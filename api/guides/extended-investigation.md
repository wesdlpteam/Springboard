# Extended Investigation (VCE study design)

Scope: a Units 3-4 only study where students develop critical-thinking and argument-evaluation skills, then design, conduct, report and orally defend a self-chosen extended research investigation.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- Extended Investigation has no Units 1 and 2. This is a Units 3-4 only VCE study; students enrol directly into Unit 3.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Thinking about arguments (Unit 3):** analyse and evaluate arguments. Key knowledge: modes of thinking/argumentation (analysis, inference, categorisation, generalisation, evaluation), elements of arguments (claims, evidence, conclusions), characteristics of valid arguments, cognitive bias. Key skills: identify argument elements, assess validity, analyse/evaluate arguments and evidence, assess potential for cognitive bias.
- **Area of Study 2 — Developing a research question (Unit 3):** propose and justify a research question and methods of investigation. Key knowledge: purpose/role of research, qualities of a good research question, strengths/weaknesses of qualitative/quantitative data methods, research ethics (consent, confidentiality, privacy), academic referencing conventions. Key skills: compare and evaluate research questions/methods, consider ethical issues, review research literature, propose and justify the question and methods, cite sources correctly.
- **Area of Study 3 — Planning and commencing the investigation (Unit 3):** write a detailed research plan, commence the investigation, and explain/justify selected research methods to an educated, non-specialist audience (oral report). Key knowledge: research project management techniques, ethical considerations, techniques for evaluating arguments/evidence in reviewed material. Key skills: write a research plan applying project management, explain/justify methods, summarise and evaluate reviewed research, critically evaluate the developing investigation.
- **Area of Study 1 — Thinking about research (Unit 4):** evaluate evidence and reasoning in the research of others and in the student's own investigation. Key knowledge: modes of argumentation (inference, inductive/deductive reasoning, synthesis, justification), cognitive bias sources, techniques for assessing soundness of research arguments and evidence quality. Key skills: analyse/evaluate arguments in research literature, detect cognitive bias, evaluate own investigation's methods/evidence/conclusions.
- **Area of Study 2 — Completing a written report (Unit 4):** complete a report for an educated, non-specialist audience that presents and evaluates the results of the investigation. Key knowledge: relevant literature for the research area, data selection/presentation techniques, synthesising findings, academic report structures, citation conventions. Key skills: manage the project to conclusion, analyse data, synthesise findings, evaluate the investigation, write in an appropriate academic style with full citations.
- **Area of Study 3 — Presenting and defending findings (Unit 4):** explain the investigation to an educated, non-specialist audience, critically evaluating the research process and defending findings (oral presentation with panel Q&A). Key knowledge: relevant literature, techniques for organising a report into an oral presentation. Key skills: present findings clearly, respond to panel questions, critically evaluate and defend the research process and conclusions.

Cross-study skills: an Extended Investigation Journal documenting critical-thinking exercises, progress and feedback runs across both units and is required for authentication; a Critical Thinking Test (external assessment) also contributes to the study score alongside the School-assessed Coursework.
