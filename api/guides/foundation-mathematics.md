# Foundation Mathematics (VCE study design)

Scope: practical, everyday mathematics for workplace, personal, community and further-learning contexts, built around number, data, finance and measurement.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Algebra, number and structure:** apply integers, fractions, decimals, ratios, proportions, percentages and rates to solve practical problems; Unit 2 extends this to constructing and manipulating formulas and symbolic expressions. Key skills: estimate and calculate by hand and with technology; check accuracy and reasonableness.
- **Area of Study 2 — Data analysis, probability and statistics:** collect, represent and interpret data from community, work, recreation and media contexts. Key skills: collect and represent data; summarise, compare and report on data sets and trends.
- **Area of Study 3 — Discrete mathematics (Financial and consumer mathematics):** calculate personal, local and national financial matters — banking, loans, fees, interest, taxation, superannuation, income and payslips; Unit 2 adds product comparison and life-stage financial planning.
- **Area of Study 4 — Space and measurement:** apply the metric system and measurement conventions to domestic, societal, industrial and commercial contexts; Unit 2 extends to shape, location, maps and travel itineraries.
- All units include a **mathematical investigation** (formulation, exploration, communication) and outcomes covering non-routine problem-solving and computational thinking/technology.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Algebra, number and structure:** extends to algorithmic/computational thinking, direct and indirect variation, transposition of formulas and simultaneous equations (e.g. break-even points) in community, business and industry contexts.
- **Area of Study 2 — Data analysis, probability and statistics:** extends to survey design, digital data representations, standard deviation, percentiles, and long-term data/relative frequency (e.g. epidemics, climate).
- **Area of Study 3 — Discrete mathematics (Financial and consumer mathematics):** extends to investment/borrowing services, business income and expenditure (invoicing, BAS, superannuation), insurance and financial/economic indicators.
- **Area of Study 4 — Space and measurement:** extends to geometric constructions, transformations, similarity and symmetry, scaling of plans/diagrams, and measurement error/tolerance/precision.
- All four areas of study are covered across Units 3 and 4 (two per unit), alongside outcomes on non-routine problem-solving and computational thinking, assessed through investigations and an end-of-year examination.

Cross-study skills: every unit blends by-hand and technology-based calculation, requires interpreting and communicating results in real-world context, and builds computational thinking as a distinct assessed outcome alongside routine and non-routine problem-solving.
