# Art Making and Exhibiting (VCE study design)

Scope: Art Making and Exhibiting develops students as practising artists and curators, exploring materials/techniques/processes in a chosen art form, making finished artworks, and researching how artworks are exhibited and cared for. Practice is recorded throughout in a Visual Arts journal.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 1 — Explore (materials, techniques, art forms):** Students experiment with materials/techniques/processes in a specific art form. Key knowledge: characteristics/properties of materials, historical development of art forms, safe practice. Key skills: investigate and manipulate materials, document exploration in the Visual Arts journal.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 2 — Expand (make, present, reflect):** Students make and present at least one finished artwork from a set theme, writing a reflection and artist's statement.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 3 — Investigate (research and present):** Students research three Australian artists (including at least one Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander artist), one artwork each, and present exhibition-style information on them.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — Understand (ideas, artworks, exhibition):** Students visit a thematic exhibition, select artworks and design their own thematic exhibition of six artworks (three from the visited show, three self-selected).
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Develop (theme, aesthetic qualities, style):** Students use art elements/principles to develop aesthetic qualities and experimental artworks responding to a set theme.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 3 — Resolve (ideas, subject matter, style):** Students refine and resolve at least one finished artwork on the selected theme, developing an emerging personal style.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Collect (inspirations, influences, images):** Students research three artists as inspiration, collecting ideas in their Visual Arts journal while experimenting with materials/techniques to develop subject matter. Key knowledge: art elements/principles/aesthetic qualities, visual language. Key skills: document and evaluate developing ideas and technique.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Extend (make, critique, reflect):** Students make artworks from their Area of Study 1 research, then present a peer critique and use feedback to plan further development. Key skills: make artworks, present and reflect on a critique.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 3 — Connect (curate, design, propose):** Students research the curator's role and exhibition spaces, then plan an exhibition proposal (with didactic information) for the same three artists studied in Area of Study 1.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Consolidate (refine and resolve):** Students refine and resolve at least one finished artwork extending Unit 3's ideas, and research conservation/care of their materials.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Present (plan and critique):** Students plan the display of their finished artwork(s) for a specific space, then present and reflect on a critique.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 3 — Conserve (present and care):** Students visit exhibitions to study presentation, conservation and care methods, applying this understanding to their own finished artwork(s).

Cross-study skills: sustained visual and written documentation of art-making practice in the Visual Arts journal, presenting and responding to peer/teacher critique, and research into real exhibitions (minimum two per year from the VCAA Exhibitions List) run through every unit.
