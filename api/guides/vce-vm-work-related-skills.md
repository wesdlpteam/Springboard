# VCE Vocational Major: Work Related Skills (VCE study design)

Scope: students build practical career-planning, employability and workplace-rights knowledge, moving from researching future careers through to workplace conduct/rights and a final job-ready portfolio.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Future careers (Unit 1):** identify and discuss likely employment growth areas using credible data and apply findings to develop strategies to improve future career prospects. Key knowledge: reliable employment information sources, labour market information (skills shortages, growth/emerging/green industries), strategies to analyse employment information, planning/decision-making strategies (SWOT, decision models). Key skills: research and evaluate employment information, propose and justify career-prospect strategies.
- **Area of Study 2 — Presentation of career and education goals (Unit 1):** forecast potential employment possibilities and evaluate education pathways supporting a selected industry growth area. Key knowledge: goal setting, personal strengths/challenges, researching and communicating findings on industry/education pathways, reflecting on feedback. Key skills: research and present career/education goals, seek and act on feedback.
- **Area of Study 1 — Skills and capabilities for employment and further education (Unit 2):** identify and evaluate individual aptitudes/interests against broad industry groups and evidence personal core skills for a chosen industry. Key knowledge: changing nature of work, employability skills (communication, teamwork, problem-solving, initiative), specialist/technical skills, personal capabilities (growth mindset, work ethic). Key skills: evaluate personal skills/capabilities, collect evidence/artefacts, propose career-prospect strategies.
- **Area of Study 2 — Transferable skills and capabilities (Unit 2):** demonstrate knowledge of recruitment/interview processes and essential/technical skills required by broader industry groups. Key knowledge: transferable skills and employability, ongoing training/development, recruitment and selection processes, resume features, job-interview preparation. Key skills: propose career strategies, collect evidence, apply knowledge to simulated resumes, applications and mock interviews.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Workplace wellbeing and personal accountability (Unit 3):** analyse and evaluate characteristics of a healthy, collaborative workplace and identify strategies to contribute to it. Key knowledge: workplace structures, statutory bodies (Safe Work Australia, WorkSafe, Fair Work Ombudsman), workplace culture and work-life balance, employer/employee expectations and rights, diversity and inclusion. Key skills: analyse and evaluate workplace environment concepts, apply knowledge to real/simulated workplace scenarios.
- **Area of Study 2 — Workplace responsibilities and rights (Unit 3):** outline the National Employment Standards and pay/conditions methods, explain workplace bullying/discrimination/sexual harassment and their legal consequences. Key knowledge: National Employment Standards, worker classifications, awards/agreements/contracts, anti-discrimination law, grievance procedures, statutory enforcement bodies. Key skills: analyse and evaluate workplace-relations concepts, propose strategies to improve the workplace environment, apply knowledge to case studies.
- **Area of Study 3 — Communication and collaboration (Unit 3):** apply questioning and listening techniques in a workplace/simulated workplace and understand networking, professional relationships and diverse teamwork. Key knowledge: team roles, workplace communication methods, active listening, benefits of diverse teams/networks, digital collaboration. Key skills: apply inclusive communication and active-listening techniques, build professional relationships and networks.
- **Area of Study 1 — Portfolio development (Unit 4):** analyse the advantages/limitations of physical, digital and/or hybrid portfolios for employment or higher-education applications. Key knowledge: portfolio purpose/audience/types, portfolio artefacts, quality characteristics, industry/tertiary practice. Key skills: propose portfolio content, compare portfolio types, research industry practice, justify portfolio choice.
- **Area of Study 2 — Portfolio presentation (Unit 4):** communicate personal skills/attributes, evaluate evidence and analyse presentation skills for future enhancement, culminating in a formal panel interview. Key knowledge: employability/personal skills, portfolio presentation methods, self-assessment and feedback strategies. Key skills: curate and present a portfolio to a target-industry panel, evaluate the portfolio and presentation.

Cross-study skills: all four units build a cumulative body of evidence and artefacts (resumes, cover letters, mock interviews, portfolio items) that culminates in the Unit 4 panel-style portfolio presentation.
