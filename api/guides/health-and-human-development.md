# Health and Human Development (VCE study design)

Scope: examines health and wellbeing as a multidimensional concept, from youth health in Australia through to global health, human development and international aid.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Concepts of health:** students explore definitions, dimensions (physical, social, emotional, mental, spiritual) and WHO prerequisites for health, and analyse sociocultural factors behind youth health variations. Key knowledge: health indicators (morbidity, mortality, life expectancy, self-assessed status), youth and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander perspectives. Key skills: describe perspectives/dimensions, draw conclusions from health data, analyse sociocultural factors.
- **Area of Study 2 — Youth health and wellbeing:** students research one youth health focus area (e.g. mental health, vaping, alcohol, gambling, relationships, safety), its impact, costs and improvement strategies. Key knowledge: risk/protective factors, healthcare services, government/community programs, advocacy opportunities. Key skills: interpret data to identify priority areas, research and analyse one focus area in depth.
- **Area of Study 3 — Health and nutrition:** students study major nutrients, food selection models (Australian Guide to Healthy Eating, Health Star Rating) and sociocultural/commercial influences on youth eating. Key knowledge: nutrient functions, consequences of nutritional imbalance, marketing tactics targeting youth. Key skills: evaluate food selection models and nutrition information sources, analyse influences on healthy eating.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 2) — Developmental transitions:** students examine the youth-to-adulthood transition, healthy relationships, parenthood, and prenatal/infancy/early-childhood development as intergenerational. Key knowledge: physical/social/emotional/intellectual development, prenatal risk/protective factors, intergenerational health. Key skills: describe lifespan stages, analyse relationship and parenthood factors, explain intergenerational health.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 2) — Youth health literacy:** students investigate Australia's health system (GPs, Medicare, PBS, NDIS) from a youth perspective, access barriers, rights/responsibilities, and digital health opportunities/challenges. Key knowledge: local health services, privacy/confidentiality, consumer complaint options. Key skills: research local services, critique health information, explain access factors and rights.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Understanding health and wellbeing:** students treat health as dynamic and subjective, using indicators (incidence, DALY, YLL, YLD, HALE, mortality) to analyse variations in Australia's health status, including contributors like smoking, alcohol and nutritional imbalance. Key knowledge: biological/sociocultural/environmental factors, health as an individual/national/global resource. Key skills: interpret and apply indicators, use data to evaluate health status, analyse variation factors.
- **Area of Study 2 — Promoting health in Australia:** students trace public health approaches since 1900 (old public health, biomedical approach, social model, Ottawa Charter), Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health programs, and Australia's health system (Medicare, PBS, NDIS). Key knowledge: reasons for health improvements, healthy-eating initiatives, challenges to nutritional change. Key skills: analyse improvement data, evaluate health promotion programs and the health system's role.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 4) — Global health and human development:** students compare health status and human development across low-, middle- and high-income countries using the Human Development Index and sustainability concepts, and analyse global trends (climate change, conflict, migration, trade, tourism, digital tech). Key knowledge: factors behind global health inequalities (water access, sanitation, poverty, discrimination), dimensions of sustainability. Key skills: evaluate the HDI, analyse inequality factors, analyse implications of global trends.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 4) — Health and the Sustainable Development Goals:** students study the UN SDGs (especially SDG 3 and its links to SDGs 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 12), the WHO's goal and objectives, and evaluate aid types and Australia's aid program. Key knowledge: bilateral/multilateral/NGO aid, features of effective aid, individual/social action opportunities. Key skills: analyse SDG relationships, evaluate aid effectiveness, discuss avenues for individual and social action.

Cross-study skills: health status is analysed through the same recurring toolkit (multiple dimensions of health, standard indicators, and biological/sociocultural/environmental factors) at every level from individual youth to global populations, so data-interpretation and factor-analysis skills built in Unit 1 carry straight through to Unit 4.
