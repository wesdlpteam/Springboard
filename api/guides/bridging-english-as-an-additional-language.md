# Bridging English as an Additional Language (VCE study design)

Scope: developing Standard Australian English (SAE) skills across everyday, academic, self-expression, media and workplace contexts for students building English proficiency.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — English for everyday purposes (Unit 1):** students engage with and produce everyday texts, making appropriate decisions for purpose, audience and context. Key knowledge: verbal and paralinguistic features of SAE (gesture, eye contact, politeness rules); idiomatic and colloquial expression; reading/listening strategies; metalanguage for discussing texts; SAE conventions of spelling, punctuation, grammar and syntax. Key skills: use pronunciation, stress and intonation appropriately; initiate/sustain/end conversations; select reading strategies for purpose; summarise and retell key points; apply SAE conventions in spoken and written texts.
- **Area of Study 2 — English for academic purposes (Unit 1):** students understand and produce print, spoken and multimodal academic texts for learning purposes. Key knowledge: subject-specific language, symbols and abbreviations across other studies; strategies for extracting relevant information; how academic texts present information in print/digital form. Key skills: identify essential language and information from academic texts; use research strategies to locate and evaluate information; make notes and summarise; use graphic organisers, tables and charts to present information.
- **Area of Study 1 — English for self-expression (Unit 2, compulsory):** students understand and produce texts for self-expression (journals, blogs, letters, poetry, scripts), making appropriate choices for purpose, audience and context. Key knowledge: how authors use language, structure and conventions for different audiences; features of self-expressive texts; techniques for creating meaning. Key skills: identify and analyse how textual features create meaning; plan, draft, revise and edit for technical accuracy; apply SAE spelling, punctuation and syntax conventions.
- **Unit 2 elective (choose one) — English in the media OR English for the workplace:** English in the media has students explain how media texts position audiences and produce texts that attempt to do the same, covering persuasive language, fact vs opinion, and metalanguage for media construction; English for the workplace has students use and produce workplace texts (résumés, emails, meeting notes, interviews), covering workplace-specific vocabulary, register and communication conventions.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
Bridging English as an Additional Language is a Units 1-2 study only; it has no Units 3-4 sequence.

Cross-study skills: all areas of study integrate listening, speaking, reading, viewing and writing, with an explicit focus on SAE conventions (spelling, punctuation, grammar, syntax) and on adjusting language choices for purpose, audience and context across everyday, academic, personal, media and workplace settings.
