# VCE Vocational Major: Literacy (VCE study design)

Scope: students build practical reading, viewing, writing and speaking skills across personal, digital, workplace and advocacy contexts, with all four units school-assessed.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Literacy for personal use (Unit 1):** demonstrate understanding of how text types are constructed for different purposes, audiences and contexts through written, digital, oral and visual responses. Key knowledge: structures/features of narrative, informative, persuasive, instructional and workplace texts, purpose/context/audience influences, plagiarism, drafting/editing/proofreading, literacy conventions. Key skills: read and annotate a range of texts, identify purpose/audience/context, research using reliable sources, plan/draft/edit responses.
- **Area of Study 2 — Understanding and creating digital texts (Unit 1):** apply understanding of literacy and digital communication conventions to respond to and create digital content suitable for community, workplace or vocational contexts. Key knowledge: structure of webpages/digital texts, digital security, copyright/attribution, safe and respectful digital practices. Key skills: read and evaluate digital texts, plan/create/edit digital content, demonstrate respectful digital interactions, reference sources correctly.
- **Area of Study 1 — Understanding issues and voices (Unit 2):** explain the purpose, audience and main ideas of diverse arguments in different text types through annotations and written/oral/multimedia responses. Key knowledge: persuasive language and visuals, elements of oral communication, bias and perspective, conventions of discussion/debate. Key skills: identify purpose/audience of persuasive content, note-take and annotate, infer meaning, compare texts, contribute to discussion.
- **Area of Study 2 — Responding to opinions (Unit 2):** interpret the values and opinions of others and present, in oral form, points of view supported by evidence. Key knowledge: structures of persuasive/influential content, logic/reasoning/emotion in argument, copyright and attribution conventions. Key skills: critically analyse arguments, select and reference supporting evidence, draft/revise/edit written and oral responses.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Accessing and understanding informational, organisational and procedural texts (Unit 3):** locate, read and understand the purpose, audience and content of informational, organisational and procedural texts through real-life documents (e.g. safety reports, tax forms, contracts). Key knowledge: structures/features of technical and organisational texts, organisation-specific language, oral communication elements. Key skills: access and interpret complex/technical documents, locate information via headings/indexes, compare texts for effectiveness, contribute to discussion.
- **Area of Study 2 — Creating and responding to organisational, informational or procedural texts (Unit 3):** create organisational, informational and procedural texts that reflect a specific workplace or vocational experience. Key knowledge: structure/language/purpose of organisational texts, characteristics of procedural texts. Key skills: explain purpose/audience of instructional texts, source reliable information, create informative/procedural/instructional content.
- **Area of Study 1 — Understanding and engaging with literacy for advocacy (Unit 4):** produce written, visual and multimodal texts for the promotion of self, a product or a chosen community group. Key knowledge: language choice and audience/purpose, visual cues and presentation, personal branding, structures of advocacy texts (webpages, brochures, social media). Key skills: identify layout/design elements, design promotional material, critically evaluate influential material, draft/edit/revise.
- **Area of Study 2 — Speaking to advise or to advocate (Unit 4):** negotiate a topic and complete an oral presentation showcasing reflections on learning (options: civic participation or personal management contexts; can connect to Work Related Skills or Personal Development Skills). Key knowledge: elements of oral communication, persuasive language techniques, copyright/attribution. Key skills: sequence and structure oral content, provide supporting evidence, use body language/eye contact/pace effectively, critically evaluate own work.

Cross-study skills: literacy conventions (punctuation, sentence structure, paragraphing, spelling) and oral communication skills (discussion, active listening, presenting) are assessed in every unit; texts are drawn from real-life, vocational and community contexts throughout.
