# Drama (VCE study design)

Scope: Drama develops students as devisers, performers and critical audiences, using play-making techniques, performance styles and conventions to create solo and ensemble work and to analyse professional performance.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Creating a devised performance:** Students devise, demonstrate and document solo and/or ensemble drama based on stimulus material (personal, cultural, community stories, possibly including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander stories). Key knowledge: character creation, storytelling, performance styles and conventions, dramatic elements, play-making techniques. Key skills: create/sustain character, manipulate dramatic elements, document play-making techniques.
- **Area of Study 2 — Presenting a devised performance:** Students perform their devised work to an audience. Key knowledge: sustaining character in performance, conventions and symbol/transformation, actor-audience relationship, production areas. Key skills: present a performance, manipulate expressive/performance skills, incorporate production areas and conventions.
- **Area of Study 3 — Analysing a devised performance:** Students describe, reflect on and analyse their own devised work. Key knowledge/skills: apply drama terminology to analyse character, play-making techniques, dramatic elements and actor-audience relationship in their own performance.
- **Area of Study 4 — Analysing and evaluating a professional performance:** Students analyse and evaluate a performance by professional (or community) drama practitioners. Key knowledge/skills: evaluate characterisation, conventions, dramatic elements, production areas and actor-audience relationship using drama terminology.
- Unit 2 repeats this same four-outcome structure with an Australian identity focus: devising, presenting, analysing and evaluating a performance based on a person/event/issue/place/text/icon from a contemporary or historical Australian context, plus analysing a professional Australian-work performance.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Devising and presenting ensemble performance:** Students collaboratively devise and present an ensemble performance drawing on one or multiple performance styles. Key knowledge: play-making techniques, symbol/transformation, dramatic elements, production areas (sustainably sourced), actor-audience relationship. Key skills: collaborate to construct, manipulate and present an ensemble performance with specific intended audience impact.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Analysing and evaluating a devised performance:** Students describe, analyse and evaluate the creation and performance of their own ensemble work using drama terminology.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 3 — Analysing and evaluating a professional drama performance:** Students analyse and evaluate a professional performance selected from the VCAA-prescribed Unit 3 Playlist, covering expressive/performance skills, conventions, dramatic elements, production areas and actor-audience relationship.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Demonstrating techniques of solo performance-making:** Students devise a short (1-2 minute) solo demonstration from a prescribed VCE solo examination structure, applying symbol and transformation of character, time and place, and explain the techniques used.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Devising a solo performance:** Students create, develop and perform an extended solo performance in response to a prescribed structure from the VCE Drama solo performance examination, applying performance styles, conventions, dramatic elements and production areas, and stating their intentions in writing.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 3 — Analysing and evaluating a devised solo performance:** Students describe, analyse and evaluate the creation, development and presentation of their own solo performance using drama terminology.

Cross-study skills: play-making techniques (extracting and shaping dramatic potential from stimulus material), manipulation of dramatic elements/conventions/production areas, symbol and transformation of character-time-place, the actor-audience relationship, and correct use of drama terminology to describe, analyse and evaluate recur across every unit and outcome.
