# Biology (VCE study design)

Scope: how living things function at cellular and system level, inherit and transmit traits, evolve, and respond to challenges such as pathogens, taught through both content and hands-on scientific investigation.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Unit 1, AoS1 — How do cells function?:** explain cellular structure/function and analyse the cell cycle, growth, death and differentiation. Key knowledge: prokaryotic vs eukaryotic cells; surface-area-to-volume ratio and organelles; membrane transport; mitosis, apoptosis and deviant cell behaviour (cancer); stem cell properties.
- **Unit 1, AoS2 — How do plant and animal systems function?:** explain cell specialisation and analyse how systems are regulated. Key knowledge: vascular plant water regulation; digestive/endocrine/excretory systems; homeostasis (temperature, blood glucose, water balance) and malfunctions.
- **Unit 1, AoS3 — scientific investigation:** students adapt or design and conduct an investigation into cell/system function, generating primary data.
- **Unit 2, AoS1 — How is inheritance explained?:** explain chromosomes, genomes, genotypes and phenotypes; predict patterns of inheritance. Key knowledge: meiosis and gametes; genotype notation, dominance/codominance; pedigree charts and monohybrid crosses.
- **Unit 2, AoS2 — How do inherited adaptations impact on diversity?:** analyse reproductive strategies and how adaptations/interdependencies enhance survival. Key knowledge: asexual vs sexual reproduction, cloning; structural/physiological/behavioural adaptations; keystone species; Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander knowledge of species adaptation.
- **Unit 2, AoS3 — bioethical issue investigation:** research into a contemporary bioethical issue in genetics, reproduction, inheritance or adaptation, using secondary data.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, AoS1 — What is the role of nucleic acids and proteins in maintaining life?:** analyse the nucleic acid-protein relationship and evaluate DNA manipulation tools. Key knowledge: DNA/RNA structure, genetic code, transcription/translation; DNA manipulation (CRISPR-Cas9, PCR, gel electrophoresis, GM organisms).
- **Unit 3, AoS2 — How are biochemical pathways regulated?:** analyse photosynthesis/cellular respiration regulation and biotechnology applications. Key knowledge: enzyme/coenzyme roles; C3/C4/CAM photosynthesis; glycolysis, Krebs cycle, electron transport chain.
- **Unit 4, AoS1 — How do organisms respond to pathogens?:** analyse the immune response and disease treatment strategies. Key knowledge: innate and adaptive immunity (B/T lymphocytes, antibodies); vaccination, herd immunity, immunotherapy.
- **Unit 4, AoS2 — How are species related over time?:** analyse genetic change in populations, speciation, relatedness between species, and human evolution. Key knowledge: allele frequency change; fossil evidence; structural/molecular homology and phylogenetic trees; hominin evolution.
- **Unit 4, AoS3 — student-designed scientific investigation:** design and conduct an investigation generating primary quantitative data, presented as a scientific poster with a logbook.
- Assessed through School-assessed Coursework across Units 3 and 4 plus an end-of-year examination worth 50 per cent of the study score.

Cross-study skills: key science skills (investigation design, data analysis, scientific evidence, science communication) are assessed alongside key knowledge every unit, with emphasis on practical work, bioethical reasoning, and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander knowledge and perspectives.
