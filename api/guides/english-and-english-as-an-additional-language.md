# English and English as an Additional Language (VCE study design)

Scope: English/EAL develops close reading and analytical writing about set texts, crafting original texts for purpose and audience, and analysing/constructing persuasive argument, with parallel (slightly adjusted) outcomes for EAL students.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 1 — Reading and exploring texts:** Students make personal connections with, and explore vocabulary/text structures/language features/ideas in, one set text. Key knowledge: inferential reading/viewing strategies, ideas/concerns/tensions in a text. Key skills: personal and analytical writing about a text, using textual evidence, conventions of Standard Australian English.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 2 — Crafting texts:** Students study three mentor texts (short stories, speeches, essays, podcasts, poetry/songs, feature articles, memoirs) and craft their own imaginative/persuasive/informative texts for a specific purpose, context and audience, with a commentary explaining their choices. Key skills: generate/draft/refine writing, apply standard and (where appropriate) non-standard language conventions.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — Reading and exploring texts:** Students explore a second set text (a different text type from Unit 1), analysing how vocabulary/text structures/language features construct meaning, including historical/social/cultural context.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Exploring argument:** Students analyse persuasive texts (print, digital, audio/audiovisual) on a contemporary local/national issue, examining contention, evidence and persuasive strategies, then construct their own point-of-view text for oral presentation.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Reading and responding to texts:** Students study one VCAA Text List text (different type from Unit 4), analysing character dynamics, ideas/concerns/conflicts, and historical/social/cultural context through sustained analytical essay writing (EAL students also complete a listening/comprehension task).
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Creating texts:** Students select one idea from the VCAA Framework of Ideas and study three matched mentor texts, then create their own effective, cohesive text for a specific purpose/context/audience, with a reflective commentary on their writing choices.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Reading and responding to texts:** Students study a second VCAA Text List text (different type from Unit 3), analysing explicit and implicit ideas/values and how context shapes reader response, in sustained analytical writing.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Analysing argument:** Students analyse argument, language and visuals in persuasive media texts (since 1 September of the previous year) on a significant national/international issue, then plan and present their own point-of-view text orally.

Cross-study skills: sustained analytical essay writing using textual evidence and appropriate metalanguage, applying the conventions of Standard Australian English syntax/punctuation/spelling, and understanding how vocabulary/text structures/language features and context shape meaning and audience positioning are consistent skills built across all four units.
