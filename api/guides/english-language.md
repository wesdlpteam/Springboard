# English Language (VCE study design)

Scope: English Language is the linguistic study of how English works as a system and how it varies, tracing its structure, acquisition, historical change and social/cultural variation in Australian society, using the subsystems of language (phonetics/phonology, morphology, lexicology, syntax, discourse, semantics, pragmatics) and metalanguage throughout.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 1 — The nature and functions of language:** Students identify and describe primary aspects of the nature and functions of human language. Key knowledge: language as a meaning-making system, the subsystems of language, influence of register/tenor/situational and cultural context. Key skills: use the subsystems and metalanguage to describe spoken/written Australian texts objectively.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 2 — Language acquisition:** Students describe types of language acquisition and discuss linguistic theories (universal grammar, usage-based accounts). Key knowledge: developmental stages, first- vs additional-language learning, the International Phonetic Alphabet. Key skills: interpret child-language samples and linguistic field-work data.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — English across time:** Students describe language change and attitudes to it (prescriptivism/descriptivism), studying one elective in depth (Incursions: Vikings/Norman conquest/religion/plagues, OR Inventions: printing press/dictionaries/typewriters/internet). Key knowledge: changes across the subsystems (e.g. the Great Vowel Shift, borrowings, semantic shift).
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Englishes in contact:** Students explain the effects of the global spread of English, including Australian Englishes, pidgins/creoles, and language maintenance/shift/reclamation with particular reference to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander languages.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Informality:** Students identify, describe and analyse distinctive features of informal spoken and written language. Key knowledge: colloquial language, slang, taboo language, discourse strategies, functions of informal language (intimacy, solidarity, in-group membership). Key skills: analyse informal texts and transcripts using metalanguage.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Formality:** Students analyse distinctive features of formal spoken and written language. Key knowledge: rhetoric, jargon, euphemism, non-discriminatory language, functions (authority, expertise, obfuscation). Key skills: analyse formal texts, including from the public domain.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Language variation in Australian society:** Students analyse varieties of English in Australia (Standard Australian English, Aboriginal Australian Englishes, migrant ethnolects, accents) and attitudes towards them, and how they reflect national identity.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Individual and group identities:** Students analyse how sociolects, idiolects and code-switching reflect and convey individual/group identity, including overt and covert prestige and social attitudes/disadvantage.

Cross-study skills: systematic, objective analysis of texts using the subsystems of language and correct metalanguage, and understanding how situational/cultural context shapes language choice, are the consistent methodological core across all four units.
