# Systems Engineering (VCE study design)

Scope: students apply the systems engineering process to research, design, produce and evaluate integrated mechanical and electrotechnological systems, with growing emphasis on ethics and sustainability.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Electrotechnological systems design and society (Unit 1):** explain developments in electrotechnology, discuss influences on design, and investigate, define, generate and design an electrotechnological system with sustainable design concepts. Key knowledge: historical/cultural influences (including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander perspectives), the systems engineering process, feedback and control systems, electrotechnological components and circuit diagrams, microcontrollers and programming. Key skills: discuss influences on engineering design, investigate and define a sustainability problem, generate and draw design options, compare components against ethical/environmental/economic impacts.
- **Area of Study 2 — Creating electrotechnological systems design (Unit 1):** use the systems engineering process to apply electrotechnological and control concepts and produce a system addressing a sustainability problem, then evaluate it. Key knowledge: planning/producing/evaluating activities, agile project management, circuit calculations (resistance, voltage, current, power), circuit simulation tools, OHS and hierarchy of control. Key skills: plan and manage production, construct/interpret circuits, measure and test system functionality, apply Ohm's Law, work safely and collaboratively.
- **Area of Study 1 — Evolution of mechanical systems design (Unit 2):** explain the evolution of mechanical systems and discuss innovation and inclusive responses to mechanical engineering design. Key knowledge: historical/cultural developments (including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander contributions), the six simple machines, inclusive design (e.g. wheelchairs, prosthetics, water purification), design thinking strategies. Key skills: explain historical/technical developments, research influences on mechanical design, justify inclusive design choices, investigate/define a problem and generate design options.
- **Area of Study 2 — Creating mechanical systems (Unit 2):** apply engineering principles and the systems engineering process to design and produce a mechanical system addressing an inclusive design problem. Key knowledge: planning/producing/evaluating activities, agile design, simple machines/gears/linkages calculations, IPO diagrams, feedback and control, fault-finding and repair, OHS hierarchy of control. Key skills: plan and manage production, represent systems diagrammatically, test and evaluate performance, perform calculations, work safely to produce a compliant system.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Integrated and controlled system principles and design (Unit 3):** investigate, analyse and apply concepts and principles to design, plan and commence production of an integrated mechanical and electrotechnological system that considers ethical design. Key knowledge: systems engineering process activities, design thinking, ethical design, feedback/control systems, diagrammatic/digital representations, hierarchy of control, engineering formulae. Key skills: investigate/define an ethically-framed problem, generate and plan a design using agile processes, perform engineering calculations, represent and simulate systems, apply project management and safety practices.
- **Area of Study 2 — Clean energy technologies (Unit 3):** discuss advantages and disadvantages of renewable and non-renewable energy sources, and analyse and critique technologies used to harness, generate and store energy. Key knowledge: renewable and non-renewable energy forms, energy life-cycle transformations, cradle-to-cradle (C2C) analysis, technologies to harness/store/transmit renewable energy, efficiency factors. Key skills: compare energy sources and batteries, discuss supply-chain challenges and transition strategies, critique solar/wind technologies against non-renewables.
- **Area of Study 1 — Producing and evaluating integrated and controlled systems (Unit 4):** complete production of the Unit 3 system, then evaluate and critique the production/test/diagnosis process and the system itself, considering ethical design. Key knowledge: Australian/international standards and data sheets, open-source model and attribution, risk assessment and hierarchy of control, OHS-compliant production, fault-finding/maintenance methods. Key skills: implement the work plan, document risk management and modifications, test/diagnose/repair the system, measure and evaluate performance, justify safety practices.
- **Area of Study 2 — New and emerging technologies (Unit 4):** evaluate a range of new and emerging systems engineering technologies and analyse/critique their likely impacts. Key knowledge: technologies developed within roughly the last eight years or still in development, across fields such as defence, aerospace, health, robotics, transport; scientific/technological/environmental/economic/social factors driving development. Key skills: research and explain how new technologies operate, speculate on impacts and worldviews, analyse and critique advantages/disadvantages.

Cross-study skills: the systems engineering process (investigate/define, generate/design, plan/produce, evaluate) and hierarchy of control for safety recur across every unit; students maintain a multimodal record of evidence throughout design and production work.
