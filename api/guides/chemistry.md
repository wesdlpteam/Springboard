# Chemistry (VCE study design)

Scope: the structure, properties and reactions of materials, water-based and energy-producing chemistry, and organic compound design, taught through content plus hands-on scientific investigation and a sustainability lens.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Unit 1, AoS1 — How do the chemical structures of materials explain their properties and reactions?:** explain how elements form carbon compounds, metallic lattices and ionic compounds; use chromatography. Key knowledge: periodic trends; covalent structures (Lewis structures, VSEPR, intermolecular forces); metallic bonding and the reactivity series; ionic formulas and precipitation reactions.
- **Unit 1, AoS2 — How are materials quantified and classified?:** calculate mole quantities, name organic compounds, explain polymer design. Key knowledge: Avogadro's constant, molar mass, empirical/molecular formulas; organic families and IUPAC naming; addition vs condensation polymers and the circular economy.
- **Unit 1, AoS3 — sustainability investigation:** research into sustainable production/use of a material, drawing on green chemistry principles and, where relevant, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander material-processing practices.
- **Unit 2, AoS1 — How do chemicals interact with water?:** explain water's properties and analyse acid-base/redox reactions. Key knowledge: hydrogen bonding; Brønsted-Lowry theory, pH and neutralisation; oxidising/reducing agents and displacement reactions.
- **Unit 2, AoS2 — How are chemicals measured and analysed?:** calculate concentrations/solubility, use volumetric analysis and stoichiometry. Key knowledge: acid-base titrations; the ideal gas equation; salt analysis via colorimetry.
- **Unit 2, AoS3 — scientific investigation:** an investigation generating primary data on gas production, acid-base/redox reactions, or water/soil analysis.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, AoS1 — What are the current and future options for supplying energy?:** compare fuels quantitatively, design/test galvanic and fuel cells. Key knowledge: fossil fuels vs biofuels; thermochemical equations; combustion stoichiometry and calorimetry; the electrochemical series and Faraday's Laws.
- **Unit 3, AoS2 — How can the rate and yield of chemical reactions be optimised?:** analyse systems for rate/yield, explain electrolysis. Key knowledge: reaction rate and catalysis; equilibrium (Le Chatelier's principle, Kc, reaction quotient Q); electrolytic/rechargeable cells and green hydrogen production.
- **Unit 4, AoS1 — How are organic compounds categorised and synthesised?:** analyse organic families, design synthesis pathways, evaluate sustainability. Key knowledge: carbon bonding and isomers; IUPAC naming; substitution, addition, esterification, hydrolysis; atom economy.
- **Unit 4, AoS2 — How are organic compounds analysed and used?:** apply qualitative/quantitative tests, deduce structures, explain medicine function. Key knowledge: mass spectrometry, IR, NMR interpretation; medicinal chemistry (chirality, enzyme denaturation, competitive inhibitors).
- **Unit 4, AoS3 — student-designed scientific investigation:** design and conduct an investigation (energy/chemical production or organic analysis/synthesis), presented as a scientific poster with a logbook.
- Assessed via School-assessed Coursework plus an end-of-year examination worth 50 per cent of the study score.

Cross-study skills: key science skills (investigation design, data analysis, scientific evidence, science communication) are assessed alongside key knowledge every unit; sustainability and green chemistry principles recur throughout, alongside strong emphasis on practical technique.
