# Literature (VCE study design)

Scope: close, critical and creative engagement with literary texts, their forms, contexts and the different ways readers interpret them.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Reading practices:** respond to a range of texts through close analysis. Key knowledge: how characters, settings and events shape reader response; how form, features and language guide meaning; how others' views can enhance a reading. Key skills: produce close-analysis responses; discuss how form/language and the reader's own context shape meaning; use textual evidence.
- **Area of Study 2 — Exploration of literary movements and genres:** explore the conventions of a distinctive movement or genre (e.g. modernism, tragedy, crime, science fiction) across one complete text and multiple samples. Key knowledge: conventions (language, settings, structures, characterisation) and how they contribute to meaning. Key skills: identify shared conventions; produce analytical and creative responses.
- **Voices of Country (Unit 2, AoS1):** explore the voices, perspectives and knowledge of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander authors and creators, including connections to Country, colonisation's impact and reconciliation. Key skills: research and reflect on these voices; produce creative/analytical responses.
- **The text in its context (Unit 2, AoS2):** analyse how a text represents a specific historical, social and cultural period. Key skills: analyse how context is represented; develop critical and analytical responses.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Adaptations and transformations:** closely analyse a set text, then discuss how meaning changes when adapted to a different form (a required sixth text, an adaptation of one of five prescribed texts). Key skills: analyse literary form and language; compare original and adapted text; produce a fluent analytical response.
- **Area of Study 2 — Developing interpretations:** develop an interpretation of a set text, then a second interpretation informed by a supplementary reading (e.g. scholarly article) that enriches or contests the first. Key skills: explore context, views and values; compare two interpretations using textual evidence.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 4) — Creative responses to texts:** construct a creative transformation of the set text and comment critically on both. Key skills: analyse form and language closely; produce a fluent creative response connected to the original.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 4) — Close analysis of texts:** scrutinise language, style and construction to build a coherent view of a whole text using detailed textual references. Key skills: analyse form, features and language and synthesise into a coherent argument.

Cross-study skills: at least one task each in Units 1-2 and 3-4 must include speaking and listening; close textual analysis, comparative/contextual reasoning and creative writing run across all four units.
