# Art Creative Practice (VCE study design)

Scope: Art Creative Practice develops students as artists who research the practices of other artists and use the "Creative Practice" (making and responding, guided by Interpretive Lenses) to explore, document and resolve their own visual ideas into a personal Body of Work.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Artists, artworks and audiences:** Students research and analyse three artists (different times/cultures, including at least one contemporary and one Australian artist in Unit 1) using the Structural and Personal Lenses (Unit 2 shifts to the Cultural Lens and requires an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander artist and a collaborating artist). Key knowledge: artists' practices, use of the lenses, visual language. Key skills: analyse and discuss artists' practices, formulate and justify personal opinions with evidence.
- **Area of Study 2 — The Creative Practice / collaborative Creative Practice:** Students experiment across at least three art forms (Unit 1) or explore collaborative practice and resolve at least one finished artwork on a social/cultural theme (Unit 2). Key knowledge: methods for experimenting with materials/techniques/processes, visual language, relationships between artist-artwork-viewer. Key skills: experiment and make personal visual responses, apply the relevant Interpretive Lens(es).
- **Area of Study 3 — Documenting and reflecting on the Creative Practice:** Students document and evaluate (Unit 2: also critique) their use of the Creative Practice and visual language. Key knowledge/skills: written and visual documentation of critical/reflective thinking, applying the Interpretive Lenses in annotations.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Investigation and presentation:** Students research one artwork by a selected artist as inspiration, then develop a personal response (a finished artwork, not a direct reinterpretation) that begins their Body of Work, documenting their exploration and presenting a critique. Key knowledge: ideas/issues explored by artists, visual language development, the Interpretive Lenses, documenting and critiquing practice.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Personal investigation using the Creative Practice:** Students continue developing the Body of Work, exploring ideas of personal interest (from Area of Study 1, other artists, or new interests), documenting and critically annotating using the Interpretive Lenses.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Documentation and critique of the Creative Practice:** Students present a critique of their Unit 3 work, then use the feedback to progressively refine, document and resolve their Body of Work, applying relevant Interpretive Lenses.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Resolution and presentation of a Body of Work:** Students refine and resolve their Body of Work's materials, techniques, processes and visual language, and consider how presentation and context communicate ideas to a viewer/audience.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 3 — Comparison of artists, their practice and their artworks:** Students research and compare at least one historical and one contemporary artist, applying all three Interpretive Lenses (Structural, Personal, Cultural) to analyse and interpret at least two artworks by each, using evidence to support interpretation.

Cross-study skills: applying the Structural, Personal and Cultural Interpretive Lenses to analyse and interpret artworks, and documenting/critiquing one's own art-making process (the Creative Practice) with appropriate art terminology, run through every unit.
