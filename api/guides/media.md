# Media (VCE study design)

Scope: Media examines how media products construct representations and narratives, how audiences engage with them, and develops students' own media production skills across pre-production, production and post-production.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 1 — Media representations:** Students explain how media representations are constructed and how audiences engage with, consume and read them. Key knowledge: media codes/conventions, audience engagement and production, social/institutional factors. Key skills: analyse codes/conventions and compare representations across products, contexts and time.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 2 — Media forms in production:** Students design, produce and evaluate media representations in two or more media forms. Key knowledge: pre-production/production/post-production stages, media technologies. Key skills: apply production techniques and codes/conventions, operate media technologies.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 3 — Australian stories:** Students analyse Australian fictional and non-fictional narratives in two or more media forms, including at least one by an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander creator. Key knowledge: narrative structure, institutional/economic/political constraints on production. Key skills: analyse narrative structures and the impact of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander voices on national identity.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — Narrative, style and genre:** Students analyse the distinctive style of media creators/producers and how genre and audience engagement shape narrative construction.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Narratives in production:** Students apply the media production process to design, produce and construct their own narratives within ethical/legal/community constraints.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 3 — Media and change:** Students discuss the influence of new media technologies on audiences, individuals, media industries and institutions, including recent social/ethical/legal issues.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Narratives and their contexts:** Students closely analyse one substantial media narrative (a feature film, hour-long audio/streaming product, photographic series or equivalent digital product) to analyse its construction, audience engagement and the social/historical/institutional/cultural/economic/political contexts shaping it and its readings.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Research, development and experimentation:** Students research their chosen media form (narrative, genre, style, codes/conventions) and complete two production experiments to develop skills, informing their production plan.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 3 — Pre-production planning:** Students develop a documented, industry-style written and visual pre-production plan (e.g. 3-10 min video/film, 10 min animation, 8+ min audio, print or photographic product) for a specified audience, carried into Unit 4.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Media production:** Students produce, refine, resolve and distribute the media product designed in Unit 3, using reflection and feedback to revise it through post-production.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Agency and control in the media:** Students use evidence and argument to discuss the changing balance of power between media and audiences, media regulation in Australia, and ethical/legal issues in the media.

Cross-study skills: use of accurate media language, analysis of media codes and narrative conventions, and understanding how social/historical/institutional/cultural/economic/political context shapes both production and audience reading are consistent across all four units.
