# Mathematical Methods (VCE study design)

Scope: study of elementary functions, algebra, calculus, and probability/statistics, and their applications in practical and theoretical contexts, preparing students for STEM, economics and related fields.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Functions, relations and graphs:** Unit 1 covers polynomial/power functions and key graph features (intercepts, domain/range, stationary points, asymptotes); Unit 2 extends to circular, exponential and logarithmic functions. Key skills: represent functions by rule/graph/table; sketch transformed and combined functions in modelling contexts.
- **Area of Study 2 — Algebra, number and structure:** spans both units. Unit 1 covers polynomial algebra (factor/remainder theorems, solving polynomial and simultaneous equations); Unit 2 covers inverse functions, exponent/log laws and Newton's method. Key skills: solve equations numerically, graphically and algebraically.
- **Area of Study 3 — Calculus:** Unit 1 introduces average/instantaneous rates of change; Unit 2 covers differentiation and anti-differentiation of polynomials and applications (stationary points, optimisation).
- **Area of Study 4 — Data analysis, probability and statistics:** Unit 1 covers sample spaces, counting principles and combinations; Unit 2 extends to conditional probability, independence and simulation.
- Both units include a **mathematical investigation** and outcomes on non-routine problem-solving and computational thinking with technology.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Functions, relations and graphs:** graphs of power, exponential, logarithmic and circular functions, transformations, and sum/difference/product/composite functions, modelling with combinations of these types.
- **Area of Study 2 — Algebra, number and structure:** composition/inverse functions, solving polynomial equations (including numerical methods), literal and simultaneous equations (including no-solution/infinite-solution cases).
- **Area of Study 3 — Calculus:** differentiation (product, chain, quotient rules) of polynomial, exponential, circular, logarithmic and power functions; anti-differentiation and integration, the trapezium rule and fundamental theorem of calculus; applications to graph sketching, optimisation and area under/between curves.
- **Area of Study 4 — Data analysis, probability and statistics:** discrete/continuous random variables and probability distributions (binomial, normal), mean/variance/standard deviation, and statistical inference for sample proportions including confidence intervals.
- Assessed via School-assessed Coursework plus an end-of-year examination; non-routine problem-solving and computational-thinking outcomes apply across all areas of study each unit.

Cross-study skills: heavy integration between algebra, functions, calculus and probability throughout, with both by-hand analytical skill and CAS/graphing technology expected, plus explicit outcomes on modelling and communicating mathematical reasoning.
