# Dance (VCE study design)

Scope: Dance develops students as choreographers, performers and dance analysts, combining safe physical technique with the study of movement, choreographic process and dance works.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Dance perspectives:** Students describe and document features of other choreographers' dance works. Key knowledge: dance terminology, intention, movement categories and physical skills, influences on choreographic choices. Key skills: identify and describe intention and movement, discuss relationships between movement choices and communication of intention.
- **Area of Study 2 — Choreography and performance:** Students choreograph and perform a solo, duo and/or group dance work and complete structured improvisations. Key knowledge: safe dance practice, the choreographic process, personal movement vocabulary, structuring a cohesive work. Key skills: improvise, research and develop an intention, structure and rehearse a work, perform expressively.
- **Area of Study 3 — Dance technique and performance:** Students safely and expressively perform a learnt solo, duo or group dance work. Key knowledge: safe dance practices, movement vocabulary and intention of a learnt work, rehearsal and performance approaches. Key skills: systematic physical training, rehearse and perform a learnt work with spatial awareness.
- **Area of Study 4 — Awareness and maintenance of the dancer's body (Unit 1 only):** Students describe health/wellbeing and physiology principles and demonstrate safe body use. Key knowledge: musculo-skeletal system, common injuries and prevention, body maintenance and wellbeing factors. Key skills: explain safe physical practice and injury prevention/management.
- Unit 2 adds elements of movement (time, space, energy), choreographic devices, dance traditions/styles (including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander and other Australian dance) and form, applied across the same three areas of study (perspectives, choreography/performance, technique/performance).

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Dance perspectives:** Students analyse two prescribed dance works (Unit 3) and one prescribed group work (Unit 4) from the VCAA Prescribed list. Key knowledge: intention, movement vocabulary, choreographic devices, form/dance design, spatial organisation and group structures (Unit 4), influences on choreographers. Key skills: analyse and discuss intention, form, dance design and influences using appropriate terminology.
- **Area of Study 2 — Choreography, performance and analysis (skills-based solo, Unit 3; solo, Unit 4):** Students choreograph, rehearse, perform and analyse their own solo dance work. Key knowledge: developing an intention, choreographic process and devices, structuring phrases/sections into form, production element choices, rehearsal/performance processes. Key skills: choreograph and structure a cohesive solo work, perform with artistry, document and analyse the realisation process.
- **Area of Study 3 — Dance technique, performance and analysis of a learnt dance work (Unit 3 only):** Students learn, rehearse, perform and analyse a duo or group dance work created by another choreographer. Key knowledge: safe dance practice for complex physical skills, the work's intention/movement vocabulary/group formations, realisation processes. Key skills: memorise and accurately execute movement, work with other dancers on timing/spacing, document and analyse the realisation process.

Cross-study skills: consistent use of correct dance terminology, safe dance practice, and documenting/analysing the choreographic and performance process (planning, rehearsal, performance) run through every area of study across all four units.
