# Foundation English (VCE study design)

Scope: building practical reading, writing, listening and speaking skills in Standard Australian English for academic, workplace and social contexts.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Reading and viewing texts (Unit 1):** students produce prose and graphic summaries and explanations of specified texts. Key knowledge: structures and features of text types (narratives, media articles, reports, blogs, films); literary/stylistic devices and vocabulary; reading strategies (skimming, scanning, paraphrasing, note-taking, summarising). Key skills: identify layout, purpose and audience of print/digital texts; paraphrase to communicate main ideas; identify opinion and bias; create graphic outlines and concept maps to summarise texts.
- **Area of Study 2 — Creating texts (Unit 1):** students produce different text types for different purposes and audiences in academic, workplace or social contexts. Key knowledge: structures and features of narratives, informative texts, arguments, letters, reports, resumes and web pages; how purpose/context/audience shape structure and language; spelling, punctuation and syntax conventions. Key skills: select appropriate text type, vocabulary, tone and style; plan, draft, revise, edit and proofread; apply Standard Australian English conventions.
- **Area of Study 3 — Listening to and presenting persuasive texts (Unit 1):** students listen, interact and speak in formal contexts for a range of audiences and persuasive purposes. Key knowledge: conventions of spoken texts (podcasts, speeches, interviews, group presentations); formal/informal features; verbal and paralinguistic cues; active-listening techniques. Key skills: comprehend and note-take from aural texts; adjust tone/register for context; convey information clearly using aids; respond to others' views in discussion.
- **Unit 2 — Thinking and learning through English** repeats the same three areas of study (reading/viewing, creating texts, listening/presenting) at a more independent level: students produce analytical or creative responses to a literary text, write coherent texts for a target audience/purpose, and present a spoken or multimodal text conveying a reasoned, informed opinion. Key knowledge/skills build on Unit 1 with more emphasis on personal response, comparative language across texts, and public speaking/debate conventions.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
Foundation English is a Units 1-2 study only; it has no Units 3-4 sequence.

Cross-study skills: all areas of study integrate reading/viewing, writing and speaking/listening around real-world academic, workplace and social texts, with an emphasis on planning-drafting-editing processes and Standard Australian English conventions throughout.
