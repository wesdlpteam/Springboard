# Texts and Traditions (VCE study design)

Scope: teaches exegesis (close textual interpretation) of sacred texts, their historical formation, social impact and later reinterpretation, using texts from Christianity, Islam and Judaism at Units 3-4.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — The importance of sacred texts to the tradition:** students study how a sacred text developed historically and came to be accepted as sacred by its tradition. Key knowledge: key moments of development and acceptance, characteristics/function of the text. Key skills: identify and explain development/acceptance, analyse the text's function in its literary and religious context.
- **Area of Study 2 — The exegesis of texts:** students learn basic exegetical method, analysing a text's original sociocultural, political, religious and historical setting. Key knowledge: original context, exegetical methods (literary context, forms, themes, meaning for original audience), scholarly commentaries. Key skills: identify contextual information, apply exegetical methods, use commentaries.
- **Area of Study 3 — Sacred texts and later traditions:** students study how a later tradition or artistic form (feasts, rituals, art, literature, film, music) has interpreted a selected sacred text. Key knowledge: later interpretations through custom or artistic media, relevant scholarly discussion. Key skills: analyse meanings attributed by the later tradition and/or artistic interpretations, summarise scholarship.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 2) — Sacred texts in the past:** students research the historical origin and development of selected texts and the social attitudes (justice, authority, environment, racism, gender) they express. Key knowledge: when/where/why/how texts took shape, historical people/places/events. Key skills: identify and explain formation, describe and analyse social attitudes in the texts.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 2) — Sacred texts today:** students examine how a tradition currently uses its sacred texts' authority to address social issues. Key knowledge: type of authority attributed today, how institutions use texts to guide work on social issues. Key skills: describe current authority, discuss the tradition's use of texts, analyse an institution's application of texts.
- **Area of Study 3 (Unit 2) — Comparing religious traditions:** students compare how sacred texts from at least two traditions address a common social issue. Key knowledge: content on the shared issue, reasons for each tradition's stance, similarities/differences, comparison challenges. Key skills: identify shared content, compare values/teachings, analyse reasons for differences, outline comparison challenges.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — The background of the tradition:** students study the sociocultural and historical conditions from which the religious tradition (Christianity, Islam or Judaism) emerged. Key knowledge: social/cultural/religious/political conditions of the foundational period, relevant historical people/places/events. Key skills: identify and explain sociocultural context and its influence, synthesise primary and secondary sources.
- **Area of Study 2 — Audience, purposes and literary aspects of the set texts:** students analyse the set text's purposes, literary structure, forms and techniques, and intended audience. Key knowledge: contemporary conditions relevant to formation, literary structure/forms/techniques, scholarly opinion. Key skills: identify structure and techniques, explain their function, use passages and scholarship to support analysis.
- **Area of Study 3 — Interpreting texts:** students apply at least two exegetical methods (sociocultural and literary criticism) to develop interpretations of the annually-prescribed passages for special study. Key knowledge: nature of exegesis, contextual placement of passages, meaning for the original community. Key skills: apply exegetical methods, discuss themes and their significance, use scholarly commentaries.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 4) — Interpreting texts:** students extend exegetical skills to cover all passages for special study in greater depth. Key knowledge: historical/sociocultural context of the passages, literary forms/techniques, meaning for the original audience. Key skills: analyse purpose-context relationships, apply exegetical methods, discuss meaning for the original audience.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 4) — Religious themes and their teaching purpose:** students study the annually-published themes as they develop through the set text and their teaching purpose for the original audience. Key knowledge: themes and their social/cultural/religious/historical context, how themes develop across the text. Key skills: describe and explain themes, analyse their importance to the original audience.
- **Area of Study 3 (Unit 4) — Themes in the later tradition and the later use of scripture:** students analyse how the tradition has reinterpreted a textual theme at a later point in history and how the original text is used to justify that interpretation. Key knowledge: later understandings/teachings on the theme, how the tradition was affected. Key skills: examine changed understandings, evaluate relevance of the original text, analyse the tradition's later use of scripture.

Cross-study skills: exegetical method (reading a passage for its historical, sociocultural and literary context, then its meaning for an audience) is introduced in Unit 1 and deepened every unit after, so lessons can build the same close-reading scaffold progressively across all four units.
