# Product Design and Technologies (VCE study design)

Scope: students use the Double Diamond design approach to research, design and make physical products that respond to real needs, opportunities and ethical considerations.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Developing and conceptualising designs (Unit 1):** apply design thinking to research, critique and communicate a response to a need or opportunity, and work collaboratively to develop graphical product concepts that address a design brief. Key knowledge: Double Diamond design approach, design briefs (need, end-user profile, function, project scope), factors that influence product design, research methods, types of drawings (visualisations, design options, working drawings). Key skills: apply design thinking strategies, construct and use research methods, formulate a design brief, develop evaluation criteria, use manual/digital drawing techniques.
- **Area of Study 2 — Generating, designing and producing (Unit 1):** work collaboratively to trial, test and use materials, tools and processes to determine a chosen product concept and produce it via a scheduled production plan, reflecting on teamwork. Key knowledge: prototyping and testing physical concepts, risk management, materials/tools/processes for a design specialisation, scheduled production plans. Key skills: conduct and evaluate tests/trials, experiment with materials/tools/processes safely, develop a final proof of concept, manage risks.
- **Area of Study 1 — Opportunities for positive impacts for end users (Unit 2):** investigate and critique existing products using factors that influence design to judge their success in supporting belonging, access, usability and/or equity. Key knowledge: designer roles across industries, market needs/opportunities, Double Diamond applied to critiquing products, evaluation criteria. Key skills: research and critique products, communicate findings multimodally, develop evaluation criteria.
- **Area of Study 2 — Designing for positive impacts for end users (Unit 2):** design and make an inclusive product that responds to an end user's need or opportunity, improving belonging, access, usability and/or equity. Key knowledge: primary/secondary research methods, ethical research, design thinking strategies, scheduled production plans, materials/tools/processes. Key skills: formulate an end-user profile, generate and justify product concepts, plan and record implementation, make safely.
- **Area of Study 3 — Cultural influences on design (Unit 2):** research and discuss how designers and end users are influenced by culture, with a focus on Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander design practices. Key knowledge: cultural influences on product design, factors shaping cultural needs/opportunities, ethical research methods. Key skills: examine products through a cultural lens, connect designer culture to design outcomes, discuss cultural influences from designer and end-user perspectives.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Influences on design, development and production of products (Unit 3):** critique examples of ethical product design and innovation within industrial settings. Key knowledge: manufacturing scales and methods, sustainability frameworks (6Rs, circular economy, cradle-to-cradle, DfD, EPR, lifecycle analysis, triple bottom line), planned obsolescence, technologies (AI, automation, CAD/CAM/CNC, 3D printing, robotics), experimental and alternative materials. Key skills: describe manufacturing processes, compare technologies across production scales, critique products against sustainability frameworks, analyse planned obsolescence.
- **Area of Study 2 — Investigating opportunities for ethical design and production (Unit 3):** investigate an ethics-related need or opportunity, formulate a design brief, research current market needs, and propose, evaluate and critique graphical product concepts. Key knowledge: Double Diamond activities, design brief elements, ethical research and IP considerations, drawing systems. Key skills: formulate an ethical design brief, use ethical research methods, develop and use evaluation criteria, produce graphical product concepts.
- **Area of Study 3 — Developing a final proof of concept for ethical production (Unit 3):** evaluate product concepts, synthesise end-user feedback to justify a final proof of concept, and plan to make the product safely. Key knowledge: physical prototyping, ethical/social/environmental/economic considerations in material selection, scheduled production plan components, risk assessment. Key skills: test and evaluate physical concepts, justify material/tool/process selections, develop a scheduled production plan including risk controls.
- **Area of Study 1 — Managing production for ethical designs (Unit 4):** implement the scheduled production plan from Unit 3, using materials, tools and processes safely and managing resources effectively. Key knowledge: risk management, goal-setting and progress monitoring, documenting decisions/modifications. Key skills: apply risk management, use processes safely, manage time/resources, record and justify modifications.
- **Area of Study 2 — Evaluation and speculative design (Unit 4):** synthesise data to evaluate a range of products and judge success, and discuss designs in relation to entrepreneurial activity, innovation and sustainability/ethics. Key knowledge: evaluation using criteria/data/feedback, speculative design thinking, future-focused and entrepreneurial design. Key skills: gather and interpret end-user feedback, evaluate products against criteria, suggest and justify product enhancements.

Cross-study skills: heavy emphasis on the Double Diamond design process (investigate/define, generate/design, evaluate, plan/manage) recurring across every unit; ethical, safe and collaborative practice underpins both design and production work.
