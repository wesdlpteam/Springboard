# General Mathematics (VCE study design)

Scope: broad, real-life applied mathematics covering data analysis, algebra/finance, functions/graphs, discrete mathematics (matrices, networks), and space/measurement.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Data analysis, probability and statistics:** Unit 1 investigates and compares single-variable data distributions (histograms, boxplots, five-number summary, standard deviation); Unit 2 investigates relationships between two numerical variables using scatterplots and a line of good fit. Key skills: construct and interpret graphical displays; calculate summary statistics; compare distributions.
- **Area of Study 2 — Algebra, number and structure (Unit 1) / Discrete mathematics (Unit 2):** Unit 1 covers arithmetic/geometric sequences, recurrence relations, and financial applications (interest, depreciation); Unit 2 covers graphs and networks (vertices, edges, Eulerian trails, shortest path, minimum spanning trees).
- **Area of Study 3 — Functions, relations and graphs:** Unit 1 covers linear functions, equations and piecewise (step) graphs; Unit 2 covers direct/inverse variation and transformation of non-linear data to linearity.
- **Area of Study 4 — Discrete mathematics (Unit 1: matrices) / Space and measurement (Unit 2):** Unit 1 covers matrix types, operations, inverses and transition matrices for population modelling; Unit 2 covers units of measurement, similarity, trigonometry (sine/cosine rule), Pythagoras' theorem and navigation.
- Both units include a **mathematical investigation** and outcomes on non-routine problem-solving/modelling and computational thinking with technology.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Data analysis, probability and statistics (Unit 3):** data distributions, association/correlation and causation, least-squares linear regression, data transformation, and time series analysis (smoothing, seasonality, forecasting).
- **Area of Study 2 — Discrete mathematics: Recursion and financial modelling (Unit 3):** recurrence relations and time-value-of-money for depreciation, compound interest, reducing-balance loans, and annuities/perpetuities.
- **Matrices (Unit 4):** matrix arithmetic, inverses, binary/permutation/communication/dominance matrices, and transition matrices for modelling population and dynamical systems.
- **Networks and decision mathematics (Unit 4):** graphs/digraphs, Eulerian trails/circuits, Hamiltonian paths, minimum spanning trees, flow problems, shortest path (Dijkstra's algorithm), matching (Hungarian algorithm) and critical path analysis/scheduling.
- Assessed through School-assessed Coursework across Units 3 and 4 plus an end-of-year examination.

Cross-study skills: heavy reliance on technology (CAS/spreadsheet/financial-modelling functionality) throughout, with consistent outcomes on defining/applying routine procedures, non-routine modelling and problem-solving, and computational thinking.
