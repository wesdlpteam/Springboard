# Food Studies (VCE study design)

Scope: explores food's historical, cultural, scientific, commercial and ethical dimensions in Australia and globally, combining practical cooking activities with analysis of food systems, nutrition and food information.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Food around the world:** students trace hunter-gatherer to agricultural to global food systems, studying one selected food-producing region (other than Australia) and adapting historical ingredients in practical activities. Key knowledge: factors behind emerging food systems/cuisines, global spread of trade commodities (spice, sugar, coffee), effects of industrialisation and globalisation. Key skills: research a selected region's food history, examine hunter-gatherer vs agricultural systems, demonstrate and critique historical ingredient adaptations.
- **Area of Study 2 — Food in Australia:** students study Australian First Peoples' food practices, colonial food security challenges, migration's influence on cuisine, and debate whether Australia has its own cuisine. Key knowledge: development of food production/processing industries, characteristics of a selected migrant cuisine, contemporary food trends and subcultures. Key skills: evaluate Indigenous foods practically, analyse immigration's effect on food tastes, construct a justified viewpoint on Australian cuisine.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 2) — Australia's food systems:** students study commercial food production (primary production, processing, retail, marketing, waste) and respond to a design brief creating a new food product. Key knowledge: food security/sovereignty/citizenship, new product development steps, food safety governance and labelling. Key skills: analyse food system opportunities/challenges, apply new-product-development steps, evaluate foods using qualitative/quantitative measures.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 2) — Food in the home:** students compare domestic vs commercial food production, adapt recipes for dietary needs, and study food science (heat transfer, functional properties of fats/protein/starch/sugar, Maillard reaction). Key knowledge: influences on home food planning, food science terminology for physical/chemical changes, entrepreneurial pathways from home to commercial settings. Key skills: design/adapt recipes for dietary requirements, apply food science terminology practically, design a food solution for a domestic/small-scale need.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — The science of food:** students study appetite/satiety physiology, digestion and macronutrient utilisation, gut microbiota's role in health, the evidence behind the Australian Dietary Guidelines, and food allergies/intolerances. Key knowledge: nutritional rationale of the Australian Guide to Healthy Eating, reasons for differing dietary requirements. Key skills: explain digestion and gut health, justify the Guidelines' scientific credibility, apply healthy eating recommendations in practical meal planning.
- **Area of Study 2 — Food choices, health and wellbeing:** students study Australian eating patterns, social factors shaping food access/choice, food's role in identity and mental health, media influence, and behavioural principles for healthy family eating. Key knowledge: political influences on food systems and sovereignty, exposure/modelling/repetition principles for children. Key skills: analyse social and media influences on food behaviour, discuss food-health links, develop a repertoire of healthy family meals practically.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 4) — Navigating food information:** students learn to assess the validity of food fads, trends and diets, food labels and marketing claims using evidence-based research principles. Key knowledge: criteria for assessing food information/supplement claims, regulatory standards for nutrition/health claims on labels. Key skills: assess information validity, analyse fad/trend nutritional efficacy, apply healthy eating recommendations practically.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 4) — Environment and ethics:** students research one in-depth debate on feeding a rising world population, covering food security/sovereignty/citizenship, sustainability of Australian primary production, and environmental effects across the food chain. Key knowledge: innovations/technologies for food security, sociocultural and ethical consumer concerns, environmental sustainability issues (fertilisers, biosecurity, climate change). Key skills: evaluate innovations' contribution to food security, debate food citizenship, research and draw conclusions on a selected food debate, apply practical skills reflecting ethical/sustainable choices.

Cross-study skills: every outcome pairs practical cooking/food activities (with reflection and records) alongside written or oral analysis, so lessons should plan hands-on food preparation together with research and evaluation tasks across all four units.
