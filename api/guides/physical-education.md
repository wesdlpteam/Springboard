# Physical Education (VCE study design)

Scope: combines biophysical study of the body in motion with the social/participatory side of physical activity, then applies that knowledge to skill analysis, energy systems and training program design.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — How does the musculoskeletal system work to produce movement?:** students study bones, joints, muscle fibres, contraction types and lever systems, plus injury causes and performance-enhancement ethics. Key knowledge: neural control of muscles, acute/chronic musculoskeletal injuries, permitted/prohibited substances. Key skills: apply anatomical terminology, analyse movements via lever systems, evaluate performance-enhancing substances.
- **Area of Study 2 — What role does the cardiorespiratory system play in movement?:** students study the heart, blood vessels, lungs and gas exchange at rest and during exercise, and the ethics of related performance enhancement. Key knowledge: stroke volume/heart rate/cardiac output relationships, thermoregulation, effects of regular aerobic exercise. Key skills: measure and analyse cardiorespiratory changes, evaluate performance-enhancing substances and their ethical/sociocultural implications.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 2) — How do physical activity, sport and exercise contribute to healthy lifestyles?:** students study types and benefits of physical activity, sociocultural enablers/barriers, the social-ecological model, and design a personalised plan following a Functional Movement Assessment. Key knowledge: physical activity guidelines, subjective/objective assessment methods, health-related fitness components. Key skills: analyse activity behaviour data, apply the social-ecological model, conduct an FMA and design a sustainable personal plan.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 2) — What are the contemporary issues associated with physical activity and sport?:** students investigate intrapersonal (injuries, mental fitness) and interpersonal (coaching, technology, equity, inclusion) issues, then research one issue in depth. Key knowledge: institutional/structural inclusion and equity (including for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Peoples), gender equity. Key skills: explain contemporary issues, collect and analyse issue-related information, propose ethical strategies for access and inclusion.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — How are movement skills improved?:** students classify movement skills, apply skill-acquisition principles (stages of learning, feedback, practice scheduling) and biomechanical principles (levers, Newton's laws, projectile motion) to improve performance. Key knowledge: skill classification types, biomechanical concepts, qualitative movement analysis stages. Key skills: analyse primary data, classify skills, apply biomechanical principles, perform qualitative movement analysis.
- **Area of Study 2 — How does the body produce energy?:** students study the three energy systems (ATP-CP, anaerobic glycolysis, aerobic), their interplay, fatigue mechanisms and recovery/nutrition strategies. Key knowledge: oxygen uptake/EPOC, acute physiological responses, energy system fuels/rate/yield. Key skills: explain oxygen supply-demand, collect and analyse physiological data, describe energy system interplay and recovery strategies.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 4) — What are the foundations of an effective training program?:** students undertake an activity analysis and select appropriate fitness tests to determine the physiological requirements of an activity. Key knowledge: activity analysis data types, fitness components, fitness testing validity/reliability/accuracy. Key skills: justify physiological requirements from activity analysis, justify fitness test selection, conduct valid/reliable fitness assessment.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 4) — How is training implemented effectively to improve fitness?:** students design, implement and evaluate training programs using training principles and methods, and explain chronic physiological adaptations. Key knowledge: training principles (frequency, intensity, specificity, overtraining, tapering, etc.), training methods, chronic adaptations (VO2 max, lactate tolerance). Key skills: design and critique a training program, analyse training data for modifications, explain chronic adaptations' effect on performance.
- **Area of Study 3 (Unit 4) — Integrated movement experiences:** students reflect on a practical activity using a reflective folio, analysing the interrelationships between skill acquisition, biomechanics, energy production and training across Units 3-4. Key knowledge: interdisciplinary connections between the four content strands and their impact on performance. Key skills: use primary data to integrate theory and practice, analyse interrelationships between skill acquisition, biomechanics, energy production and training.

Cross-study skills: nearly every outcome requires collecting and analysing primary data from participation in practical activity to justify a theoretical claim (about muscles, energy systems, skill, or training), so practical sessions and data-based written analysis should be planned together from Unit 1 through Unit 4.
