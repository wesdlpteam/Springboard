# Structured Workplace Learning Recognition for VET (VCE study design)

Scope: a Units 1-2 only study where students plan, undertake and reflect on two Structured Workplace Learning (SWL) placements (minimum 80 hours each, in different workplace settings) aligned with their VCE VET qualification.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Plan and prepare for Structured Workplace Learning (Unit 1):** investigate SWL options related to the student's VET qualification, describe individual skills/interests/transferable skills and career plan, and explain workplace WHS practices. Key knowledge: relationship between VET qualification and career pathways, employment sources (SWL portal, agencies, networks), SWL application/placement processes, learner profile development, WHS and emergency procedures, PPE, workplace hazards, industry-specific terminology. Key skills: investigate SWL options, use ICT to research opportunities, communicate/interview effectively, describe skills and career plans, explain WHS practices.
- **Area of Study 2 — Develop employability skills through SWL (Unit 1):** complete at least 80 hours of SWL placement, then describe skills acquired, how they relate to VET units of competency, and changes needed to improve employability skills. Key knowledge: workplace structure/operation and stakeholders, workplace tasks/processes, time management and behaviour expectations, communication/feedback methods, employability skills, links between VET competencies and workplace observations. Key skills: identify stakeholder needs, use workplace terminology, describe tools/equipment/technology used, record and explain skills/knowledge acquired, respond to supervisor feedback.
- **Area of Study 1 — Participate in Structured Workplace Learning (Unit 2):** identify different workplace types linked to the VET qualification and apply industry/workplace knowledge and transferable skills in a second SWL placement in a different setting. Key knowledge: VET qualification's relationship to future career options, sources of employment opportunities, application/interview processes for a new placement, WHS training and laws in the new setting, employer expectations. Key skills: identify and compare workplace types, apply ICT and communication skills to secure and start the placement, compare knowledge/skills application across settings.
- **Area of Study 2 — Evaluate participation in Structured Workplace Learning (Unit 2):** complete at least 80 hours of SWL placement in the different setting, apply and compare industry/workplace knowledge and transferable skills, and describe employability improvements and future career goals. Key knowledge: workplace structure/operation and digital competencies in the new setting, communication methods with stakeholders and documents, employability skills, feedback methods, links between workplace and VET competencies, future industry directions. Key skills: apply and compare knowledge/skills across the two settings, use appropriate workplace communication, apply and respond to feedback, evaluate learning and set future career goals.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- Structured Workplace Learning Recognition for VET has no Units 3 and 4. This is a Units 1-2 only VCE study.

Cross-study skills: each unit requires a separate Workplace Learning Record for authentication and assessment; students must complete each placement in a different workplace setting or context, so Unit 2 explicitly compares and consolidates against the Unit 1 experience.
