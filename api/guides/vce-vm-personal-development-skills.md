# VCE Vocational Major: Personal Development Skills (VCE study design)

Scope: students build emotional intelligence, community awareness, leadership and teamwork skills, culminating in Unit 4 with a self-directed, extended community project.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Personal identity and emotional intelligence (Unit 1):** explain and discuss personal identity and emotional intelligence, applying learnt strategies independently or collaboratively. Key knowledge: personal identity across contexts, elements of emotional intelligence (self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, social skills), strategies such as resilience and conflict resolution. Key skills: apply emotional-intelligence elements, communication/critical-thinking/problem-solving skills, discuss and evaluate key concepts.
- **Area of Study 2 — Community health and wellbeing (Unit 1):** plan, implement and evaluate an individual or group activity to improve health and wellbeing. Key knowledge: health and wellbeing concepts, factors affecting wellbeing, characteristics of inclusive communities, requirements for community/voluntary activities. Key skills: propose and justify an activity, apply planning and metacognitive skills to design/implement it.
- **Area of Study 3 — Promoting a healthy life (Unit 1):** analyse technology's impact on health and wellbeing and plan/implement/evaluate a health-promotion activity. Key knowledge: effects of technology on individuals/society, reliability/validity of health information, health-promotion programs. Key skills: critically analyse health information, design and evaluate a health-promotion activity.
- **Area of Study 1 — What is community? (Unit 2):** describe concepts of citizenship/community, analyse factors influencing community formation and promote community participation. Key knowledge: community at local/national/global levels, roles/rights/responsibilities of citizens, community engagement, social/cultural/economic influences. Key skills: analyse community formation, apply strategies to promote participation.
- **Area of Study 2 — Community cohesion (Unit 2):** identify community issues/challenges, analyse diverse perspectives and apply problem-solving strategies. Key knowledge: challenges facing communities, differing perspectives, impact on social cohesion, barriers/enablers to problem-solving. Key skills: analyse perspectives and issue impacts, apply and evaluate cohesion strategies.
- **Area of Study 3 — Engaging and supporting community (Unit 2):** discuss community engagement as an approach to addressing issues, and design/implement/evaluate a community engagement activity. Key knowledge: concept and features of effective community engagement, benefits/challenges. Key skills: propose and justify an activity, apply planning/metacognitive skills to develop and evaluate it.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Social awareness and interpersonal skills (Unit 3):** apply social awareness and interpersonal skills in a real-life or simulated scenario. Key knowledge: characteristics of social awareness, interpersonal skills (communication, negotiation, conflict resolution), digital-technology contexts, effective leadership traits. Key skills: compare/analyse influences and contexts, apply and evaluate strategies including in digital settings.
- **Area of Study 2 — Effective leadership (Unit 3):** describe effective leadership, analyse leadership qualities/styles and apply leadership skills in a real-life or simulated scenario. Key knowledge: leadership characteristics and styles (autocratic, charismatic, transformational, distributed, laissez-faire), ethics and democracy, fostering innovation. Key skills: analyse and evaluate leadership styles, apply leadership in practice.
- **Area of Study 3 — Effective teamwork (Unit 3):** describe characteristics of effective teams and evaluate personal contribution through a team activity. Key knowledge: attributes of team leaders/members, characteristics of effective teamwork, problem-solving steps, metacognitive reflection strategies. Key skills: analyse personal contribution to teamwork, apply problem-solving and reflection when working in a team.
- **Area of Study 1 — Planning a community project (Unit 4):** investigate and analyse a significant environmental, cultural, economic or social issue and plan a community project addressing it. Key knowledge: project design process, resourcing/budget/timeline, stakeholder engagement, risk/contingency planning. Key skills: analyse considerations for selecting an issue, design and document a project plan.
- **Area of Study 2 — Implementing a community project (Unit 4):** use project-planning skills to implement a timely, affordable and effective response to the chosen community issue. Key knowledge: emotional intelligence/leadership/teamwork elements in implementation, health/safety/ethical considerations, stakeholder and time management, evidence collection. Key skills: apply key elements/considerations, implement the project, document evidence.
- **Area of Study 3 — Evaluating a community project (Unit 4):** evaluate the effectiveness of the project's planning and implementation and present findings to a relevant audience. Key knowledge: evaluation components, metacognitive/reflective processes, communication strategies for target audiences. Key skills: identify strengths/weaknesses/opportunities, communicate evaluation findings in an appropriate presentation format.

Cross-study skills: communication, critical thinking, problem-solving, decision-making, planning and metacognitive skills recur as explicit key skills in every area of study across all four units, building toward the self-directed Unit 4 community project.
