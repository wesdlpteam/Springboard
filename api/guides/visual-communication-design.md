# Visual Communication Design (VCE study design)

Scope: Visual Communication Design (VCD) develops students as designers who apply the VCD design process (Discover, Define, Develop, Deliver) and divergent/convergent thinking to solve human-centred communication, object, environmental and interactive design problems.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 1 — Reframing design problems:** Students use human-centred research methods to identify a design problem and write a brief defining a communication need. Key knowledge: conceptions of good design, the Discover/Define stages, ethical research methods. Key skills: collaborate to reframe problems, compose a brief.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 2 — Solving communication design problems:** Students develop visual language (identity, colour, typography) for a brand/business using the Develop and Deliver stages. Key knowledge: design elements/principles, Gestalt principles, copyright/IP obligations. Key skills: ideation sketching, present concepts for critique, resolve a design solution.
- **Unit 1, Area of Study 3 — Design's influence and influences on design:** Students design a sustainable three-dimensional object, considering economic/technological/cultural/environmental/social influences and circular design practices. Key skills: 2D/3D drawing methods (technical flats, orthogonal projection, paraline/perspective), rendering, technical drawing conventions.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — Design, place and time:** Students design an environment (architecture/interior/landscape), drawing inspiration from historical and contemporary design styles, presenting a model or documentation drawings to Australian Standards.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Cultural ownership and design:** Students learn ethical/legal responsibilities around Indigenous knowledge (including the Australian Indigenous Design Charter) and design original personal iconography.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 3 — Designing interactive experiences:** Students design a user interface (UX) for a digital product, using wireframes, typographic conventions and Gestalt principles, presented as static visuals (no functioning prototype required).

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Professional design practice:** Students research and compare how contemporary designers work in one or more fields, and complete at least two practical design exercises applying relevant methods/media/materials. Key knowledge: designer roles/relationships/responsibilities, ethical/legal obligations, sustainable practice.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Design analysis:** Students compare and analyse two or more design examples, evaluating how design elements/principles and aesthetic decisions address audience, purpose and context.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 3 — Design process: defining problems and developing ideas:** Students apply the Discover, Define and Develop phases to a real/fictional client brief defining two distinct communication needs, generating and critiquing design ideas for each (carried into Unit 4).
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Design process: refining and resolving design concepts:** Students apply the Deliver phase, refining and testing concepts for each communication need through an iterative cycle, then devise and deliver a pitch justifying their design decisions.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Presenting design solutions:** Students produce and present a final, resolved design solution for each of the two communication needs, using visual language, methods, media and materials appropriate to the brief.

Cross-study skills: applying the full VCD design process (Discover-Define-Develop-Deliver) with divergent and convergent thinking, using design elements/principles and appropriate design terminology in annotations, and giving/responding to design critiques recur through every unit.
