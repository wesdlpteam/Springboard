# Business Management (VCE study design)

Scope: how a business is planned, established, managed and transformed, from the initial idea through staffing, operations and change management.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — The business idea:** students describe how a business idea is created and developed. Key knowledge: entrepreneurship, personal motivations for starting a business, characteristics of successful entrepreneurs, sources of business opportunity, market research, contribution of businesses to national economic and social wellbeing.
- **Area of Study 2 — Internal business environment and planning:** students analyse internal factors affecting planning. Key knowledge: business structures and models, buying vs establishing a business, resource needs, location, sources of finance, support services, SWOT analysis, business plans, corporate social responsibility.
- **Area of Study 3 — External business environment and planning:** students explain macro and operating factors affecting planning. Key knowledge: legal/political, social, economic, technological and global factors; customer needs, competitors, suppliers, special interest groups.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — Legal requirements and financial considerations:** students outline requirements for establishing a business. Key knowledge: registration and compliance, role of external professionals, banking and record-keeping, choosing suppliers, policies and procedures.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Marketing a business:** students analyse marketing and public relations strategies. Key knowledge: market research, target markets, the 7Ps model, product life cycle, customer relations, technology in marketing, public relations, corporate social responsibility.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 3 — Staffing a business:** students evaluate staff-management strategies. Key knowledge: staffing needs, job analysis and design, recruitment and selection methods, National Employment Standards, employer/employee expectations, OH&S and EEO, induction.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Business foundations:** students analyse business types, stakeholders, management styles and corporate culture. Key knowledge: business types and objectives, stakeholder interests and conflicts, management styles (autocratic to laissez-faire), management skills, corporate culture.
- **Area of Study 2 — Human resource management:** students explain motivation theories and evaluate employee-management strategies. Key knowledge: Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs, Locke and Latham's Goal Setting Theory, Lawrence and Nohria's Four Drive Theory, motivation and training strategies, performance management, termination, workplace relations participants, dispute resolution.
- **Area of Study 3 — Operations management:** students analyse operations and propose efficiency/effectiveness strategies. Key knowledge: inputs-processes-outputs, technology, materials management (JIT, MRP), quality (TQM), waste minimisation, lean management, global sourcing.
- **Area of Study 1 — Reviewing performance, the need for change:** students explain drivers of business change. Key knowledge: proactive vs reactive change, key performance indicators, Lewin's Force Field Analysis, driving and restraining forces, Porter's Generic Strategies.
- **Area of Study 2 — Implementing change:** students evaluate change-management strategies and leadership. Key knowledge: management strategies for change, Senge's Learning Organisation, low- and high-risk strategies to overcome resistance, Lewin's Three-step Change Model, effects of change on stakeholders, corporate social responsibility.

Cross-study skills: applying business management concepts and terminology, and analysing contemporary Australian/global case studies (from the past four years) against theory, run through every unit.
