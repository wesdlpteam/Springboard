# Theatre Studies (VCE study design)

Scope: Theatre Studies focuses on interpreting and producing published scripts through the production roles of acting, direction and design, and on analysing professional theatre in performance.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Exploring theatre styles/movements:** Students identify and describe distinguishing features of two or more theatre styles (Unit 1: pre-1945; Unit 2: contemporary, 1945-present, including at least one Australian play) and their scripts. Key knowledge: conventions, historical/social/political contexts, dramaturgy. Key skills: apply production roles and elements of theatre composition to scripts, convey historical/contemporary contexts.
- **Area of Study 2 — Interpreting scripts:** Students work in at least two production roles (actor, director, designer) to interpret scripts from two or more theatre styles/movements. Key knowledge: conventions, dramaturgy, recontextualisation, audience culture, safe/ethical/sustainable working practices. Key skills: interpret scripts, apply theatre technologies and terminology, demonstrate understanding of audience and audience culture.
- **Area of Study 3 — Analysing a theatre production in performance:** Students attend and analyse a live professional performance (Unit 1: pre-1945 where possible; Unit 2: analyse and evaluate a 1945-present production). Key knowledge: performance analysis approaches, actor-audience relationships, use of theatre technologies and elements of theatre composition. Key skills: analyse how meaning is constructed for an audience and how contexts/styles are realised in performance.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — Staging theatre:** Students interpret a school-selected script across the three stages of the production process (planning, development, presentation), specialising in two production roles within a collaborative production team. Key knowledge: the three-stage production process, dramaturgy, elements of theatre composition, safe/ethical/sustainable practice. Key skills: contribute effectively to two production roles, document and evaluate contributions across the process.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — Interpreting a script:** Students analyse theatrical possibilities of excerpts from a different, previously unstudied script, formulating and justifying interpretive concepts without full staging. Key knowledge/skills: dramaturgy, recontextualisation, how production roles could realise ideas, justified in writing/discussion.
- **Unit 3, Area of Study 3 — Analysing and evaluating theatre:** Students attend, analyse and evaluate a production from the VCAA Prescribed Theatre Studies Playlist, examining how the written script was interpreted through acting, direction, design and theatre technologies.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — Dramaturgical research and presenting theatrical possibilities:** Students research and justify an interpretation of a prescribed scene and monologue (from the annual VCAA monologue examination list), working towards production roles of actor/director or designer.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — Interpreting a monologue:** Students interpret and present the monologue in performance using two production roles, and orally justify their interpretive decisions.
- **Unit 4, Area of Study 3 — Analysing and evaluating a performance:** Students attend and analyse a second Playlist production (different from Unit 3), evaluating acting, direction and design and the actor-audience relationship.

Cross-study skills: dramaturgy (researching a script's context and intended meaning), working across the acting/directing/design production roles, applying elements of theatre composition and theatre technologies, and using correct theatre terminology to describe, analyse and evaluate are consistent threads through all four units.
