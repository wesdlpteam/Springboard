# Legal Studies (VCE study design)

Scope: how laws are made, applied and reformed in Victoria and Australia, from legal foundations and criminal/civil concepts through to the criminal and civil justice systems, law-making and reform.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Legal foundations:** students describe sources and types of law and evaluate effectiveness. Key knowledge: the role of laws and legal system in social cohesion, principles of justice (fairness, equality, access), characteristics of an effective law, parliament and courts in law-making, sources of law (common/statute), the Victorian court hierarchy, criminal vs civil law.
- **Area of Study 2 — Proving guilt:** students explain criminal law concepts and argue culpability in scenarios. Key knowledge: purposes of criminal law, the presumption of innocence, elements of a crime (actus reus/mens rea), strict liability, burden/standard of proof, types of crime, summary vs indictable offences, two criminal offences in detail.
- **Area of Study 3 — Sanctions:** students explain the determination of a criminal case and discuss sanctions. Key knowledge: institutions enforcing criminal law, difficulties faced by different groups, purposes and types of sanctions, sentencing factors, alternative approaches (Drug Court, Koori Courts, diversion).
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — Civil liability:** students explain civil law and argue liability in scenarios. Key knowledge: purposes and types of civil law, key concepts (breach, causation, loss, limitation of actions), two areas of civil law in detail.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Remedies:** students explain civil dispute resolution and discuss remedies. Key knowledge: dispute resolution methods (mediation, conciliation, arbitration), institutions (tribunals, ombudsmen), difficulties faced by different groups, purposes and types of remedies (damages, injunctions).
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 3 — Human rights:** students explain a contemporary human rights issue and evaluate rights protection. Key knowledge: meaning and development of human rights, protection mechanisms in Australia (Constitution, Charter, statute/common law), one human rights issue and a related case study.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — The Victorian criminal justice system:** students explain key principles, discuss sanctions and evaluate the achievement of justice principles. Key knowledge: summary vs indictable offences, burden/standard of proof, presumption of innocence, rights of the accused and of victims, Victoria Legal Aid and community legal centres, plea negotiations, court hierarchy, roles of key personnel, sentencing purposes and factors.
- **Area of Study 2 — The Victorian civil justice system:** students explain key principles, discuss remedies and evaluate the achievement of justice principles. Key knowledge: burden/standard of proof, factors before initiating a claim, dispute resolution methods and institutions (Consumer Affairs Victoria, VCAT, courts), roles of key personnel, class actions, damages and injunctions.
- **Area of Study 1 — The people and the law-makers:** students discuss parliament and court law-making and evaluate constitutional checks. Key knowledge: roles of Crown and Houses of Parliament, law-making powers (exclusive, concurrent, residual), section 109, one High Court case, factors affecting parliament and courts, doctrine of precedent, statutory interpretation, separation of powers.
- **Area of Study 2 — The people and reform:** students explain reasons for law and constitutional reform and evaluate the influence of individuals and reform bodies. Key knowledge: means of influencing law reform (petitions, demonstrations, courts, media), the Victorian Law Reform Commission, Royal Commissions and parliamentary committees, requirements for constitutional referenda, the 1967 and 2023 referendums.

Cross-study skills: defining and applying legal terminology, and synthesising legal principles to actual and/or hypothetical scenarios, run through every unit; Units 1-2 draw on criminal/civil cases from the past four years.
