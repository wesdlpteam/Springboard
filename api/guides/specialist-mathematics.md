# Specialist Mathematics (VCE study design)

Scope: in-depth mathematics emphasising mathematical structure, modelling, problem-solving, reasoning and proof, extending Mathematical Methods content into advanced algebra, calculus, vectors and discrete mathematics.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Algebra, number and structure (Unit 1: Proof and number, graph theory, logic):** covers number systems, set notation, primes, proof techniques (contradiction, contrapositive, induction); graphs (vertices/edges, planar graphs, Euler's formula, trails, Hamiltonian paths); propositions, truth tables, Boolean algebra and algorithm design. Key skills: construct proofs and arguments; use graphs to model situations; test propositions with truth tables.
- **Area of Study 2 — Discrete mathematics (Unit 1):** sequences/series, first-order recurrence relations, combinatorics (pigeon-hole principle, permutations/combinations) and matrices. Key skills: solve recurrence problems; apply counting techniques; use matrix algebra.
- **Area of Study 1 — Data analysis, probability and statistics (Unit 2):** distributions of sums of discrete random variables, simulation and sampling distributions of the sample mean.
- **Area of Study 2 — Space and measurement (Unit 2):** trigonometry and identities, transformations of the plane, and vectors in the plane (addition, dot product, geometric proofs, motion applications).
- **Area of Study 3/4 (Unit 2) — Complex numbers; Functions, relations and graphs:** complex number arithmetic and polar form; partial fractions, reciprocal/inverse circular functions, and locus definitions of conics. Both units include a **mathematical investigation** and outcomes on non-routine problem-solving and computational thinking.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Discrete mathematics (Logic and proof):** conjecture, quantifiers and proof techniques (direct, cases, contradiction, contrapositive, induction), applied across the other areas.
- **Area of Study 2 — Functions, relations and graphs:** rational and quotient functions, partial fraction decomposition, and analysis of asymptotes, stationary points and inflection.
- **Area of Study 3 — Algebra, number and structure (Complex numbers):** De Moivre's theorem, roots of unity, factorisation over C, and the fundamental theorem of algebra.
- **Area of Study 4 — Calculus:** advanced differentiation (inverse circular functions, second derivatives, implicit differentiation, related rates) and integration (substitution, partial fractions, integration by parts), applications to area/volume, differential equations and kinematics.
- **Area of Study 5 — Space and measurement (Vectors):** vector algebra, dependence/independence, dot/cross products, vector proofs, vector/Cartesian equations of lines and planes, and vector calculus for motion.
- **Area of Study 6 — Data analysis, probability and statistics:** distributions of linear combinations of random variables, the sample mean distribution, confidence intervals and hypothesis testing for a population mean.
- Assessed via School-assessed Coursework plus an end-of-year examination; each unit's outcomes require defining/applying key concepts, non-routine investigation, and computational thinking.

Cross-study skills: rigorous proof runs throughout, not just in the dedicated logic area; students move fluently between algebraic, graphical, numerical and vector representations, using CAS/technology alongside by-hand work.
