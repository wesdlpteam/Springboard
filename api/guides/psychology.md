# Psychology (VCE study design)

Scope: how biological, psychological and social factors shape thoughts, emotions, behaviour and mental wellbeing, from brain function through to sleep, stress, learning, memory and mental health.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — What influences psychological development?:** students discuss the complexity of psychological development over the life span. Key knowledge: hereditary and environmental influences, the biopsychosocial approach, emotional/cognitive/social development, sensitive and critical periods, typical vs atypical behaviour, neurodiversity, and the role of mental health professionals.
- **Area of Study 2 — How are mental processes and behaviour influenced by the brain?:** students analyse the brain's role in mental processes. Key knowledge: hindbrain, midbrain and forebrain functions, brain plasticity, effects of acquired brain injury and chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE).
- **Area of Study 3 — How does contemporary psychology conduct and validate research?:** a student-directed investigation evaluating a piece of contemporary psychological research. Key knowledge: primary vs secondary data, evidence quality, science communication, reproducibility and validity of research.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — How are people influenced to behave in particular ways?:** students analyse social cognition. Key knowledge: person perception, attributions, attitudes, stereotypes, cognitive bias and dissonance, heuristics, prejudice/discrimination/stigma, obedience, conformity and media influence.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — What influences a person's perception of the world?:** students compare visual and gustatory perception. Key knowledge: attention, top-down/bottom-up processing, biological/psychological/social influences on perception, perceptual distortions (illusions, agnosia, synaesthesia).
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 3 — student-adapted/designed investigation:** students conduct a practical investigation into internal or external influences on perception and/or behaviour, generating and analysing primary data.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — How does the nervous system enable psychological functioning?:** students analyse nervous system function and stress. Key knowledge: central/peripheral nervous system subdivisions, neurotransmitters vs neuromodulators, synaptic plasticity, stress as a psychobiological process (fight-flight-freeze, cortisol, gut-brain axis), Selye's General Adaptation Syndrome, the Transactional Model of Stress and Coping, coping strategies.
- **Area of Study 2 — How do people learn and remember?:** students apply models of learning and discuss memory. Key knowledge: classical and operant conditioning, observational learning, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander ways of knowing, the Atkinson-Shiffrin multi-store model, brain regions in implicit/explicit memory, episodic/semantic memory, mnemonics.
- **Area of Study 1 — How does sleep affect mental processes and behaviour?:** students analyse the demand for sleep and effects of disruption. Key knowledge: REM/NREM sleep, circadian and ultradian rhythms, sleep measurement (EEG, EMG, EOG), sleep deprivation effects, circadian rhythm disorders, sleep hygiene and zeitgebers.
- **Area of Study 2 — What influences mental wellbeing?:** students apply a biopsychosocial approach to specific phobia. Key knowledge: levels of functioning, resilience, social and emotional wellbeing (SEWB), mental wellbeing as a continuum, biological/psychological/social causes and treatments of phobia, protective factors, cultural determinants for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples.
- **Area of Study 3 — How is scientific inquiry used to investigate mental processes and psychological functioning?:** students design and conduct their own practical investigation with primary quantitative data, presenting findings as a scientific poster (aim, method, results, discussion, conclusion) supported by a logbook.

Cross-study skills: practical/laboratory work, logbook record-keeping, and evaluation of research validity run through every unit; Unit 4's investigation is assessed via a structured scientific poster (max 600 words).
