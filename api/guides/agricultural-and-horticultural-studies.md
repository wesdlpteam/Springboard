# Agricultural and Horticultural Studies (VCE study design)

Scope: covers Australia's food and fibre industries from career pathways and plant/animal production through to innovation, biosecurity and sustainable land/business management, with hands-on practical tasks throughout.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Food and fibre industries:** students study Victoria's First Peoples' and settlers' use of land, key food/fibre sectors, climate zones, and drivers of change (innovation, consumer demand, ethics, global markets) and current trends (automation, agroecology, urban agriculture). Key knowledge: influences on industry location, changing career pathways. Key skills: analyse climate/soil/sociocultural influences on industry location, describe trends, demonstrate practical analysis of production conditions.
- **Area of Study 2 — Food and fibre production:** students study systems (inputs/processes/outputs), sustainability considerations, health and safety, soil quality/testing, alternative growing media (hydroponics, aquaponics) and plant/animal selection. Key knowledge: production cycles, soil improvement techniques, scientific methodology for quality assurance. Key skills: test soil quality, justify plant/animal selection and growing media, demonstrate data collection in practical tasks.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 2) — Plant nutrition, growth and reproduction:** students study plant structure/function (photosynthesis, respiration, transpiration), growth stages, macro/micronutrients, and sexual/asexual reproduction and breeding. Key knowledge: climatic/seasonal influences, technology and post-harvest processing. Key skills: compare reproduction methods, justify gene selection for breeding, demonstrate propagation and growth monitoring.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 2) — Animal nutrition, growth and reproduction:** students study extensive vs intensive production, digestive systems (ruminant, monogastric, avian), reproductive systems/technologies and genetics/breeding programs. Key knowledge: nutrient requirements across growth stages, reproductive cycle manipulation. Key skills: compare production methods and digestive systems, evaluate reproductive technologies, demonstrate animal management practical tasks.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Innovations and solutions:** students study the role of innovation/technology in agriculture and horticulture, current research projects (climate change, food waste, population growth), and past initiatives including unforeseen consequences. Key knowledge: techniques for assessing innovation effectiveness, ethical/sustainable production debates (GMOs, animal welfare, pesticides). Key skills: evaluate new innovations and past initiatives, justify industry responses to consumer demands, apply innovative processes practically.
- **Area of Study 2 — Risks and resilience:** students study biosecurity, specific Victorian pests/diseases/weeds (e.g. aphids, footrot, gorse), integrated pest and weed management, and biological resistance to herbicides/pesticides/antibiotics. Key knowledge: prevention/control strategies, national and property biosecurity laws. Key skills: apply integrated pest/weed management, analyse solutions to biological resistance, explain biosecurity measures and laws.
- **Area of Study 1 (Unit 4) — Sustainable land management:** students study climate change impacts on production, environmental degradation types (erosion, salinity, compaction), water quality indicators, biodiversity and environmental monitoring techniques. Key knowledge: sustainable strategies for land/water/energy management, environmental control/modification techniques. Key skills: analyse climate change impacts, evaluate property management and degradation prevention, demonstrate environmental monitoring.
- **Area of Study 2 (Unit 4) — Sustainable business practices:** students study social/economic/environmental sustainability across the food and fibre supply chain (provenance, carbon footprint, food safety, commodity prices), marketing strategies, quality assurance and relevant regulation. Key knowledge: value-adding and market-broadening strategies, government regulations (e.g. Environment Protection Authority Act, OH&S Act). Key skills: evaluate business practices against sustainability dimensions, analyse marketing strategies, explain quality assurance and regulatory frameworks.

Cross-study skills: every unit pairs theoretical content with mandatory hands-on practical tasks (soil testing, propagation, animal management, pest/weed management, sustainable land/business practices), so lessons should plan practical demonstration alongside written analysis from Unit 1 through Unit 4.
