# Industry and Enterprise (VCE study design)

Scope: how workplace participation, enterprising behaviour and industry innovation develop through structured workplace learning, from entry-level work skills through to enterprise culture and industry change.

## Units 1-2 (Year 11)
- **Area of Study 1 — Contributing to the workforce:** students explain the importance of a skilled workforce and investigate career pathways. Key knowledge: value of a skilled workforce, training providers (TAFE, universities), values and attitudes to work, industry growth areas and employment trends, career goal-setting techniques, labour market data, OH&S entry requirements.
- **Area of Study 2 — Developing work-related skills:** students explain entry-level requirements and self-assess work performance, applied through at least 35 hours of structured workplace learning. Key knowledge: range of work settings (for-profit, not-for-profit), work as productive activity, job tasks and processes, entry-level requirements for two industries, self-assessment tools.
- **Area of Study 3 — Workplace effectiveness:** students explain OH&S and other workplace issues and how work-related skills address them. Key knowledge: workplace rights and responsibilities, OH&S requirements, equal employment opportunity, the role of unions, interpersonal/communication strategies.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 1 — Enterprising individuals and leadership:** students discuss enterprising behaviour and its relationship to leadership. Key knowledge: characteristics of enterprising individuals, strategies to develop enterprising behaviour, characteristics of successful leaders, leadership behaviour (mentoring, coaching).
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 2 — Enterprise and innovation in industry:** students evaluate enterprising behaviour and innovation within a selected industry. Key knowledge: innovation and competitiveness, industry classification (ANZSIC), industry stakeholders, statistical profiles, recent people-based and technology-based innovations.
- **Unit 2, Area of Study 3 — Industry issues:** students analyse two significant issues facing Australian industry (e.g. globalisation, technology, skills shortages, free-trade agreements, social/environmental issues). Key knowledge: impact of issues on industry, enterprising responses, problem-solving processes, changing role of stakeholders.

## Units 3-4 (Year 12)
- **Area of Study 1 — Enterprise culture:** students describe and evaluate enterprise culture in a community/work setting via structured workplace learning. Key knowledge: enterprise culture and its role, stakeholder behaviour, leadership and teamwork, enterprising approaches to safety, impact of individual work-related skills on culture.
- **Area of Study 2 — Creating an enterprise culture:** students discuss and evaluate four forces for change: management of quality, workplace flexibility, technology, and training/workplace learning. Key knowledge: quality control and assurance, numerical/functional flexibility, work-related technologies, formal/informal training, and the relationship of each force to work-related skills.
- **Area of Study 1 — The need for change:** students analyse pressures and opportunities driving change in a selected Australian industry over the last four years. Key knowledge: government's role in promoting change, international competitiveness, changing societal values, environmental sustainability, industry responses and their impact on stakeholders.
- **Area of Study 2 — Innovation:** students evaluate innovation in a selected Australian industry. Key knowledge: people-based and technology-focused innovation, role of research and development, government policy supporting innovation, relationships between technology, training and innovation, links to enterprise culture.

Cross-study skills: structured workplace learning (at least 35 hours per unit at Units 1-3) underpins every area of study; students maintain a workplace learning journal and/or enterprise project evaluation throughout the course.
